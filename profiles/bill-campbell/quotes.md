# Bill Campbell — Quotes

The following quotes are drawn from primary and well-documented secondary sources. Quotes in *Trillion Dollar Coach* (2019) are reported speech — Campbell's words as recalled and recorded by Eric Schmidt, Jonathan Rosenberg, and Alan Eagle, based on interviews with over eighty people who knew him. Quotes verified in contemporaneous interviews or profiles are noted accordingly. No quotes have been fabricated or paraphrased into quotation form.

---

> "Your title makes you a manager. Your people make you a leader."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019)

---

> "The purpose of a company is to bring a product vision to life. All the other components are in service to product."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019)

---

> "Leadership is about recognizing that there's a greatness in everyone, and your job is to create an environment where that greatness can emerge."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019)

---

> "The path to success in a fast-moving, highly competitive, technology-driven business world is to form high-performing teams and give them the resources and freedom to do great things."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019)

---

> "If you've been blessed, be a blessing."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019)

---

> "I hate consensus!"

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019) — characterising Campbell's objection to decision-making by committee

---

> "Being a good coach is essential to being a good manager and leader. Coaching is no longer a specialty; you cannot be a good manager without being a good coach."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019)

---

> "I look at all the people who've worked for me, or I've helped in some way, and I count up how many are great leaders now. That's how I measure success."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019); also cited in Leadership Now blog, 36 Lessons (2019)

---

> "Strive to win, but always win right, with commitment, teamwork, and integrity."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019)

---

> "There's not a product idea that I'll ever have that's going to amount to anything. But what I'll do is make sure that the right people are in the room and that the lunatic fringe has an opportunity to contribute."

— Fortune, "The Secret Coach," July 2008 (contemporaneous interview/profile)

---

> "I don't think a marketing person would have created the Macintosh, but a good marketing person would have made it better."

— Contemporaneous interview, cited in StartupArchive.org (2024) and Fortune "The Secret Coach" (2008), regarding Campbell's disagreement with Steve Jobs about market research

---

> "Always build communities. Build communities inside and outside of work. A place is much stronger when people are connected."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019)

---

> "If you ever tell an engineer at Intuit which features you want, I'm going to throw you out on the street."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019) — Campbell instructing Intuit business leaders on how to work with engineers

---

> "Positive human values generate positive business outcomes."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019)

---

> "When you listen to people, they feel valued."

— *Trillion Dollar Coach*, Schmidt, Rosenberg, Eagle (HarperBusiness, 2019)

---

*Note on sourcing: Campbell was famously private and rarely gave formal interviews. The great majority of documented Campbell quotes come from *Trillion Dollar Coach*, which was compiled from interviews with eighty-plus people who knew and worked with him. Eric Schmidt described Campbell as his "closest confidant" and the book is the most authoritative primary source available.*
