# Zach Gage

## Who He Is

Zach Gage (born 1985, Brooklyn, New York) is one of the most respected and articulate independent game designers working today. Raised in Westchester, New York in an artistic family — his mother is a painter, his grandmother created posters for Broadway shows, his grandfather was an art director — Gage came to game design through conceptual art rather than through the games industry. He holds a BA in studio art from Skidmore College (2008) and an MFA from Parsons School of Design (2010), where he focused on interactive systems, data, and internet relationships.

Gage lives and works in New York City. He describes himself as a game designer, programmer, educator, and conceptual artist. He is known by the Twitter/X handle @helvetica.

## Key Works

His catalog spans over a decade of iOS and cross-platform games:

- **SpellTower** (2011) — a word-elimination puzzle game that became his commercial breakthrough; declined multiple venture capital offers after its success
- **Sage Solitaire** (2015) — a single-player solitaire variant built for phone play
- **Really Bad Chess** (2016) — chess with randomly assigned pieces, making the game accessible to non-experts
- **Typeshift** (2017) — a word game reimagining anagrams and crosswords for the digital era, created in partnership with Merriam-Webster
- **Flipflop Solitaire** (2017) — a variation on Spider Solitaire
- **Pocket-Run Pool** (2018) — a billiards game redesigned around mobile scoring mechanics
- **Good Sudoku** (2020) — Sudoku redesigned to remove busywork and reveal the puzzle's underlying depth; created with Jack Schlesinger
- **Knotwords** (2022) — a word puzzle that blends crosswords, anagrams, and logic, created with Jack Schlesinger
- **Puzzmo** (2023) — a web-based daily puzzle platform co-created with engineer Orta Therox, acquired by Hearst Newspapers at launch

Earlier in his career, he also served as iOS developer on **Ridiculous Fishing** (2013, Vlambeer), which won an Apple Design Award, and contributed to **Card of Darkness** (2019, Zach Gage × Cartoon Network × Pendleton Ward).

## Core Philosophy

Gage is known for a specific and repeatable design method: take a familiar or classic game, understand deeply what makes it enjoyable (and what creates unnecessary friction), then rebuild it so that the essential fun is more accessible without sacrificing depth. He is not a genre inventor — he is a genre clarifier. His games do not ask "what if we made a new kind of game?" They ask "what if we made this existing game the way it was always supposed to be played?"

His art background instilled a belief that interaction itself is the medium. From his early conceptual game *Lose/Lose* (2009), which permanently deleted real files from a player's hard drive when they shot aliens, to Puzzmo's magazine-style layout, every design decision is a statement about what games are for and what they owe their players.

## Why He Replaced Coleplay in This Profile System

Coleplay documented a particular strain of viral, shareability-driven mobile design. Zach Gage represents a complementary but distinct tradition: depth-first, player-respecting, aesthetically serious indie mobile game design. He is among the most publicly articulate designers working in the premium and subscription mobile space. His extensive public record — GDC talks, podcast appearances, Twitter, published interviews — makes him an unusually reliable advisory voice on questions of game feel, ethical monetization, accessible systems design, and the business of staying independent.
