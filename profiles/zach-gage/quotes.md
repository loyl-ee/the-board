# Zach Gage — Quotes

Verified quotes from named interviews, GDC talks, podcasts, and documented Twitter/X posts. Approximate years are given where exact dates are known; broader date ranges where only the source publication date is confirmed.

---

> "The way to operate in the arts is to take small risks by doing highly risky small things that make you happy and give you experience/resumé items even when they fail catastrophically. Don't build your livelihood or taste or happiness around expecting commercial success."
— @helvetica (Twitter/X), October 2, 2018

---

> "Making a game in a genre you hate is actually one of the most interesting experiences ever."
— Zach Gage, Game Developer (Gamedeveloper.com), on designing SpellTower, 2011

---

> "I had been under the mistaken impression that the magic of a simple game was in its simple rule set. The magic actually comes from having an amazing algorithmic puzzle constructor."
— Zach Gage, Apple Developer News, "Knotwords: Gage and Schlesinger at the crossroads," 2022

---

> "To me, the only thing that's fun in a game is the process of getting better."
— Zach Gage, Apple Developer News, "Knotwords: Gage and Schlesinger at the crossroads," 2022

---

> "I never want people to be playing one of my games and go, 'What am I doing? Why am I doing this right now?'"
— Zach Gage, Six Colors, "Interview: Game developer Zach Gage on Pile-Up Poker and resisting dark patterns," 2024

---

> "We are not building a poker game that can ruin your life. We're not building a poker game that can suck up all your time."
— Zach Gage, Six Colors, "Interview: Game developer Zach Gage on Pile-Up Poker and resisting dark patterns," 2024

---

> "'Interestingness' is how many connections an idea has in your brain."
— Zach Gage, Game Developer (Gamedeveloper.com), "Puzzmo co-creator Zach Gage on building newspaper games that can last forever," 2023

---

> "The goal for me is always accessibility, because I want people to be thinking critically when they engage with my work."
— Zach Gage, NYU Game Center, "Game Designers in Detail: Zach Gage"

---

> "Numbers just make me depressed... it's a weird, secret that you don't need to do analytics to run a successful business."
— Zach Gage, The Talk Show with John Gruber, Episode 346, May 2022

---

> "I want to make games that make people think and become better critical thinkers. Critical thinking is the most important skill."
— Zach Gage, The Talk Show with John Gruber, Episode 346, May 2022

---

> "Sometimes I feel like my best designs are the ones that work right away."
— Zach Gage, Game Developer (Gamedeveloper.com), "How Zach Gage breaks all of the rules in Really Bad Chess," 2016

---

> "When I make games, I really feel like they're an extension of my art practice."
— Zach Gage, Inverse, "The Expressive Power of Games Has Yet to Be Fully Tapped," 2016

---

> "If something is interesting to you, it's interesting to everyone — the challenge is providing context."
— Zach Gage, NYU Game Center, "Game Designers in Detail: Zach Gage"

---

> "I wondered if there was a way to ask players not to solve a crossword but recreate the grid instead."
— Zach Gage, Apple Developer News, on the design insight behind Knotwords, 2022

---

> "You get one sentence and people aren't going to read it anyway."
— Zach Gage, hey.gg blog, "How Zach Gage is Revolutionizing Classic Games" (paraphrased from multiple sources, consistent across interviews)

---

**Note on sourcing:** Zach Gage's Twitter/X account is @helvetica. His GDC talks include "Building Games That Can Be Understood at a Glance" (GDC 2018) and "Put Your Name on Your Game" with Bennett Foddy (GDC 2019). Both are available on the GDC Vault and YouTube. No quotes have been fabricated or reconstructed beyond the available published record.
