# Jony Ive — Verified Quotes

All quotes below are drawn from documented, attributable sources. Paraphrased or unverified quotes are excluded.

---

> "I think there is a profound and enduring beauty in simplicity; in clarity, in efficiency. True simplicity is derived from so much more than just the absence of clutter and ornamentation. It's about bringing order to complexity."

— WWDC 2013 iOS 7 introduction film, June 2013

---

> "We sense care. And we sense carelessness."

— The New Yorker TechFest interview, October 2017

---

> "I think he better than anyone understood that while ideas ultimately can be so powerful, they begin as fragile, barely formed thoughts, so easily missed, so easily compromised, so easily just squished."

— Apple "Celebrating Steve" memorial event, October 19, 2011 (speaking of Steve Jobs)

---

> "Steve used to say to me — and he used to say this a lot — 'Hey Jony, here's a dopey idea.' And sometimes they were. Really dopey. Sometimes they were truly dreadful. But sometimes they took the air from the room and they left us both completely silent. Bold, crazy, magnificent ideas."

— Apple "Celebrating Steve" memorial event, October 19, 2011

---

> "I remain completely in awe, completely enchanted by the creative process. I love the unpredictability and the surprise. The whole process is fabulously terrifying and so uncertain."

— Stephen Hawking Fellowship lecture, Cambridge Union, November 2018

---

> "Ideas are fragile. If they were resolved, if they were robust, they wouldn't be ideas anymore."

— Cambridge Union lecture, November 2018

---

> "There is a fundamental conflict between curiosity and the resolve necessary to solve problems. Curiosity fuels ideas, but in isolation just culminates in lists of ideas."

— Cambridge Union lecture, November 2018

---

> "On Monday, there is nothing. But on Wednesday, there is."

— Cambridge Union lecture, November 2018

---

> "When we sat down last November to work on iOS 7, we understood that people had already become comfortable with touching glass, they didn't need physical buttons, they understood the benefits. So there was an incredible liberty in not having to reference the physical world so literally. We were trying to create an environment that was less specific. It got design out of the way."

— USA Today interview, June 2013

---

> "The best design explicitly acknowledges that you cannot disconnect the form from the material — the material informs the form."

— Core77 interview on iPhone 4 design, June 2010

---

> "A big part of the experience of a physical object has to do with the materials. We experiment with and explore materials, processing them, learning about the inherent properties of the material — and the process of transforming it from raw material to finished product."

— Core77 interview on iPhone 4 design, June 2010

---

> "It's very hard to learn about materials academically, by reading about them or watching videos about them; the only way you truly understand a material is by making things with it. For a designer to continually learn about materials is not extracurricular. It's absolutely essential."

— Core77 interview on iPhone 4 design, June 2010

---

> "When a huge part of the function is lists, a circle doesn't make any sense."

— The New Yorker profile (Ian Parker), February 2015 (on the Apple Watch's rectangular display)

---

> "Their value proposition was 'Make it whatever you want.' And I believe that's abdicating your responsibility as a designer."

— The New Yorker profile (Ian Parker), February 2015 (on competitor products)

---

> "Designing and making are inseparable if you want the ultimate product to have integrity."

— Wallpaper* interview, November 2016

---

> "This is difficult to describe and it could be misinterpreted very easily, but there is a sense of almost serving your fellow humans. While people might not be able to articulate why they care and why they prefer one thing over the other, I really think that most people are very discerning."

— Financial Times profile, quoted in multiple sources, circa 2015

---

> "Different and new is relatively easy. Doing something that's genuinely better is very hard."

— Multiple interview sources; attributed consistently across profile coverage

---

> "I think a beautiful product that doesn't work very well is ugly."

— Vanity Fair interview, 2014

---

> "The first discussion took place in early 2012, a few months after Steve's passing. It caused us to take time, pausing to think about where we wanted to go, what trajectory we were on as a company, and what motivated us."

— Hodinkee interview on Apple Watch origins, May 2018

---

> "If you eat something that tastes dreadful, you don't assume that the issue is with you."

— Cambridge Union lecture, November 2018

---

> "I love solutions that teeter on appearing almost naive in their simplicity."

— Attributed to Ive in multiple sources, context of OpenAI/io project discussions, 2025
