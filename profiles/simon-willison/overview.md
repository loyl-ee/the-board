# Simon Willison — Overview

## Who He Is

Simon Willison is a British software developer based in San Francisco, California. Born in January 1981, he is one of the most prolific and consequential practitioner-writers in the world of open source software and applied AI. He co-created Django (the Python web framework) in 2003–2004 while on an industrial placement at the Lawrence Journal-World newspaper in Kansas, alongside Adrian Holovaty, Jacob Kaplan-Moss, and Wilson Miner. Django now powers a substantial fraction of the world's Python web applications.

He has been blogging continuously at simonwillison.net since June 2002 — more than two decades of daily writing. This blog is one of the most closely read technical blogs among developers working at the intersection of open source, data, and AI.

## Career Arc

Willison studied Computer Science at the University of Bath. After co-creating Django, he worked at Yahoo and later as a software architect at The Guardian newspaper in the UK, where he led technical work on data journalism projects including the Guardian's MP expenses crowdsourcing effort. He co-founded Lanyrd (a social conference directory) with his wife Natalie Downe in 2010, which went through Y Combinator and was acquired by Eventbrite in 2013. He relocated to San Francisco through that acquisition and served as an Engineering Director at Eventbrite for several years.

He then spent a year as a JSK Journalism Fellow at Stanford University (2019–2020), building Datasette and articulating a vision for open source data journalism tools. After the fellowship ended, he went fully independent — self-employed, working full-time on his open source ecosystem.

He is currently a board member of the Python Software Foundation.

## Key Projects

- **Datasette** — An open source multi-tool for exploring and publishing structured data, built on SQLite. Used by journalists, archivists, researchers, and governments worldwide.
- **LLM** — A command-line tool and Python library for accessing large language models from any provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, local models via Ollama) through a unified plugin-based interface. Released April 2023.
- **sqlite-utils** — A Python CLI and library for manipulating SQLite databases, widely used for data wrangling.
- **shot-scraper** — A CLI tool for taking automated screenshots of websites, used for documentation and archival.
- **files-to-prompt** — A tool for combining files into a prompt for LLMs, built in a single session with Claude 3 Opus.

## Why He Is on This Team

Simon Willison occupies a unique position: he is the foremost practitioner-documenter of actually building things with LLMs, not theorizing about them. He does not work at an AI lab. He is not a researcher. He is a working developer who has used LLMs daily since 2022, has written thousands of words about what works and what does not, coined the term "prompt injection" to describe the central security vulnerability in AI systems, and ships real tools used by real people.

His value on this advisory team is empirical and practical. He has more documented, first-hand experience building with LLMs than almost any single developer outside the major labs — and he has shared all of it publicly in real time. When a question arises about whether an AI-powered feature is worth building, whether it is dangerous, or how to structure a prompt, Willison's body of work is among the first places to look.
