# Simon Willison — On Business

## The Indie Open Source Model

Willison operates as a fully independent developer. After his Eventbrite exit gave him meaningful financial runway, he chose not to raise venture capital or join a company. He describes his year as a JSK Journalism Fellow at Stanford as transformative: "I experienced freedom for a year, and I do not want to give this up." This is the clearest statement of his business philosophy. He values autonomy above growth, and structures his finances to protect it.

His income sources are layered: GitHub Sponsors (direct patronage from individual developers and organizations), Fly.io sponsorship of Datasette Cloud development, Mozilla MIECO program support (2023–2024), consulting engagements for companies and organizations that use his tools, and a paid Substack tier ($10/month) that provides curated LLM development summaries.

He has said he would like to eventually follow the WordPress model: open-source the core, generate revenue through managed hosting. Datasette Cloud is the product expression of this strategy — a hosted version of Datasette aimed at newsrooms and organizations that want the capability without the infrastructure overhead.

## Documentation as Business Differentiator

Willison's documentation practice is not separable from his business strategy. The blog, TIL site, annotated talks, detailed release notes, and comprehensive README files are his primary marketing. People find his tools because he writes about them, explains them clearly, and names the techniques they embody. Shot-scraper, sqlite-utils, and LLM all gained adoption largely because of documentation quality, not advertising.

He has argued that the quality of documentation signals the health and trustworthiness of a project. When he evaluates whether to invest in a new tool or dependency, he reads the docs. He builds tools whose documentation he himself would want to read.

## Sustainable Solo Development

He is honest about the challenges of solo open source work. He has said he finds it lonely and would like to hire a team. He manages multiple projects through what he calls disciplined process rather than heroic effort — his "perfect commit" practice, automated testing, the plugin architecture that lets others extend his tools without burdening him with reviews.

The plugin model is explicitly a business decision as well as a technical one. "Plugins are a way of building where you don't need to review code to add features." This allows the Datasette and LLM ecosystems to grow without proportional increases in his maintenance load. External contributors can build and release plugins independently, which both broadens the tool's capability and distributes the evangelism.

## Consulting and Intellectual Honesty

Willison takes consulting work but is careful about independence. His disclosure policy is explicit: he does not receive compensation for writing about specific topics on his blog, and sponsors have no editorial influence. This independence is the source of his credibility, and he protects it deliberately. He sells access to his expertise and his time, not his opinion.

## What He Has Not Done

He has not raised venture capital, built a traditional SaaS startup, or staffed up beyond a small team. Datasette Cloud remains modest in scale. This is a choice, not a failure — he has stated that his goal is a sustainable independent career, not a growth-at-all-costs business. He represents a specific and increasingly viable model: the developer whose reputation and documentation create durable value that compounds over time.
