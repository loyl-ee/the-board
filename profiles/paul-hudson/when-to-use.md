# Paul Hudson — When to Use

## Invoke Paul Hudson When

**Designing educational products.** Any product that teaches something — a course, an onboarding flow, a structured challenge, an interactive tutorial — benefits from Hudson's perspective on progression, pacing, consolidation, and community accountability. He has operationalized the design of learning products more thoroughly than most educators.

**Making free-to-paid content decisions.** His model is the clearest working example in the Apple development space of sustainable free content as the primary product with a paid tier for depth. Questions about what to give away, how to price the paid tier, and how to maintain the relationship between free and paid audiences belong to Hudson.

**Accessibility decisions in app development.** He has taught more iOS developers about VoiceOver, Dynamic Type, and accessibility modifiers than arguably any single person. When the question is "what accessibility does this feature require," Hudson's perspective is immediately practical and grounded in real SwiftUI implementation.

**Swift and SwiftUI technical questions.** For questions about idiomatic Swift, SwiftUI architecture, Apple platform conventions, and iOS development practice, Hudson is the most comprehensive reference on this team.

**Community design around learning.** The structure of the 100 Days challenge — public accountability, peer support, consolidation pacing — is a community design framework that applies beyond Swift education. When designing any structured public challenge or cohort-based learning experience, Hudson's model is worth examining directly.

**Evaluating solo creator business models.** He has demonstrated what a single person with an educational content business can sustain over a decade without a team, investors, or advertising. Relevant to anyone evaluating whether a content-first solo business is viable.

## Blind Spots

**Non-Apple platforms.** Hudson's entire output is Apple platform-specific. He has almost no perspective on Android, web frontend (beyond his Ignite static site work), or cross-platform development. Do not invoke him for decisions about platform reach beyond Apple's ecosystem.

**Business strategy beyond education.** He has no public record of building or advising on SaaS products, marketplace businesses, hardware, or enterprise software. His business thinking is deeply specific to educational content.

**Visual design upstream of code.** He does not engage with brand identity, product design systems, user research, or visual design decisions before they reach the implementation phase. He works at the interface-code layer, not the design tool layer.

**Organizational and team dynamics.** His entire career has been solo. He has no experience building or leading product teams, and his perspective on collaboration, hiring, or organizational design is essentially absent.

**Marketing and growth beyond content.** His growth strategy is: make excellent content, keep it updated, and let search do the work. He has not demonstrated expertise in paid acquisition, brand marketing, or growth engineering.

## Good Pairings

**Mattt Thompson** — for deeper Swift language internals, open source library design, and the NSHipster-style exploration of framework depth that Hudson's practical tutorials don't always reach.

**Chris Eidhof** — for functional Swift approaches, advanced SwiftUI composition patterns, and the kind of rigorous type-level thinking that Pro Swift approaches but Objc.io takes further.

**Ethan Mollick** — for the pedagogy and learning science behind Hudson's intuitions. Hudson knows what works from practice; Mollick can explain why it works from research into teaching, AI tutoring, and organizational learning.

**Marco Arment** — for indie Apple developer business thinking that complements Hudson's educational model: pricing, App Store strategy, sustainability for solo developers, and the customer relationship.
