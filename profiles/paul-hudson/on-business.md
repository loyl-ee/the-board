# Paul Hudson — On Business

## The Model: Free as the Foundation

Paul Hudson's business rests on a structural inversion of most content companies. The free tier is not a limited preview — it is the core product. Hackingwithswift.com contains hundreds of complete tutorials, free books (SwiftUI by Example runs to over 600 pages), the full 100 Days of Swift and 100 Days of SwiftUI courses, the Swift Knowledge Base with hundreds of code examples, interview question banks, and the community forums. All of this is free, without requiring an account, without advertising that compromises the content.

The site launched in 2014 and by 2019 was serving over 16 million page views annually — growing year-over-year users by 85% and sessions by 79%. The site's traffic comes overwhelmingly from Google search, meaning the free content strategy is also the SEO strategy. A tutorial that answers a specific Swift question — written clearly, updated for each new Swift version — ranks because it is genuinely useful, not because it has been optimized for keywords.

## Hacking with Swift+: The Paid Tier

The paid tier, Hacking with Swift+, is structured as a subscription priced at approximately $20 per month (with annual and multi-year options). It delivers:

- Advanced video tutorials and articles not available free
- Coverage of topics like custom SwiftUI components and high-performance development
- The Swift Career Accelerator (launched September 2024) — described as "the most comprehensive, career-transforming learning resource ever created for iOS development," organized across five career stages
- Access to an online reading library
- Recorded event workshops

The paid tier's proposition is clear: if the free content helped you and you want to go deeper, this is how you pay Hudson for his time. It is positioned as going further rather than unlocking the basics.

The Swift Career Accelerator specifically is the most ambitious paid product he has built — a structured learning path from beginner to lead developer, pulling together years of content and reorganizing it as a career progression tool.

## Solo Operation and What That Means

Hudson runs the entire operation alone. There is no editorial team, no video production team, no social media manager. He writes, records, codes, publishes, maintains, and supports everything himself. This is a deliberate constraint. It means everything reflects a single consistent perspective. It means quality control is personal. It means response times to questions are limited by one person's hours.

The business model works as a solo operation because the free content scales independently of Hudson's hourly availability once published. A tutorial written in 2018 still receives traffic in 2026. Books updated for Swift 6 continue selling. The leverage is in the catalogue, not the live labor.

## Books on Gumroad: The Catalogue Revenue Stream

Beyond the subscription, Hudson sells individual books directly through Gumroad. The catalogue covers iOS, macOS, watchOS, tvOS, Vapor, Swift language topics, and bundled packs. His consistent policy: when you buy a book, you receive lifetime free updates. This is explicitly presented as a philosophical stance, not just a feature — "when you trust me with your learning, I want to go above and beyond what you expect." (x.com/twostraws, September 2024)

This policy makes a single purchase decision much easier: a buyer doesn't have to calculate whether to rebuy the book when Swift 6 drops. The trust is baked into the transaction.

## Charity as Business Value

Hacking with Swift Live, his online conference, donates all profits to charity. In 2020 this generated $38,000 for a charitable cause. In 2023 proceeds went to Julian House. This is not purely altruistic — it is also a positioning decision. It signals that the event is not primarily a revenue vehicle. It gives potential attendees a second reason to participate beyond their own benefit.

## The Relationship Between Free and Paid

The core tension in any free content business is cannibalization — does giving away the basics reduce paid conversion? Hudson's model resolves this through volume and trust accumulation. The free content builds an enormous audience. A fraction of that audience upgrades to paid because they trust Hudson enough to invest. The free content is also the best marketing for the paid tier because every learner who succeeds with the free content becomes a word-of-mouth advocate.

He has never used urgency tactics, artificial scarcity, or hard-sell marketing. The conversion mechanism is simply: if the free material helped you, here is more.
