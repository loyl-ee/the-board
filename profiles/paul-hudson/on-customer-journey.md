# Paul Hudson — On Customer Journey

## The Free Entry Point: No Barrier, No Account Required

The customer journey for anyone who encounters Paul Hudson's work begins with zero friction. Tutorials on hackingwithswift.com are fully accessible without registration. The 100 Days of SwiftUI can be started immediately by visiting the site. SwiftUI by Example — over 600 pages of working code and explanations — is readable for free. There is no email capture required to begin.

This is a deliberate design decision. Hudson's stated model for supporting the site asks only three things: buy one of his books if you find the content useful, add a link to the site if you have a website, or recommend the site on social media. None of these require commitment before the learner has received value. Trust is built through the content itself, not through a relationship cultivated before access is granted.

## Discovery: Search as the Primary Funnel

Most learners find Hacking with Swift through Google. The site's tutorials are among the top results for Swift and SwiftUI questions because they are comprehensive, frequently updated, and written to answer the specific questions developers actually search for. A developer stuck on a specific problem finds a clear, working answer — and enters the Hudson ecosystem through a single article.

The solocoder.com podcast noted that Hacking with Swift ranks in the top five Google results for Swift-related searches, which represents organic traffic at a scale that would cost significant paid marketing budget to replicate through advertising.

## The 100 Days Community: Accountability and Belonging

The 100 Days challenges create a distinct community experience. The requirement to post daily progress publicly — to Twitter/X, Mastodon, or the platform of the learner's choice — means learners are not just students of Hudson's curriculum but participants in a visible, ongoing public event. Thousands of developers have shared progress under hashtags associated with the challenge, creating a searchable record of community participation and making the challenge visible to people who had not heard of it.

Hudson explicitly positions himself as present and reachable within this community. He has encouraged learners to tweet questions at @twostraws directly, committing to help when he can. At the scale he operates, he cannot answer every message, but the posture — an author who is reachable, not invisible behind a support desk — is itself part of the learner relationship.

## The Hacking with Swift Forums

The Hacking with Swift forums serve as the persistent community infrastructure. Learners ask questions, share completed projects, get unstuck, and support each other. The forums are organized by topic (SwiftUI, Swift language, specific books) and the 100 Days challenge has its own dedicated area where participants post daily updates and help each other.

This is significant: the community support mechanism is peer-to-peer rather than entirely dependent on Hudson himself. He has created conditions in which learners help each other, which scales in a way that a single creator's availability does not.

## The Paid Tier as Deepening, Not Gating

For learners who want to go further, Hacking with Swift+ and the Gumroad book catalogue represent the next stage. The transition from free to paid is not presented as a paywall reveal — it is the natural next step for someone who has exhausted what the free content offers and trusts Hudson enough to pay for more.

The Swift Career Accelerator (included in Hacking with Swift+) was designed specifically to serve this stage: a learner who has completed the 100 Days or equivalent and wants to go from beginner to employable developer to senior developer. It organizes five career stages with material appropriate to each.

## Lifetime Updates as Retention

For anyone who has purchased a book, free lifetime updates are the retention mechanism. A buyer who purchased Pro Swift in 2016 has received free updates through every major Swift version for nearly a decade. This is not standard industry practice. The effect is that the relationship with a book buyer does not end at purchase — it continues each time Hudson releases a meaningful update and the buyer receives new value from their original investment.
