# Paul Hudson

## Who He Is

Paul Hudson is a British developer, writer, and educator best known as the creator of Hacking with Swift (hackingwithswift.com), widely described as the world's largest site dedicated to teaching developers how to build apps with Swift and SwiftUI. Since launching the site in 2014 — shortly after Apple released Swift — Hudson has grown it into the defining free resource for iOS and Apple platform development education, serving over 2 million page views per month and reaching more than 20,000 users every day by 2019.

He operates entirely as a solo creator, writing every tutorial, recording every video, authoring every book, and maintaining the site's infrastructure without a team. This is not a company. It is one person who chose to make Swift education a full-time commitment.

## What He Has Built

Hudson has authored more than 20 books covering Swift, SwiftUI, iOS, macOS, watchOS, tvOS, Vapor, and related topics. Key titles include:

- **Hacking with iOS** — the original project-based book series that started the site
- **SwiftUI by Example** — a free, comprehensive reference covering hundreds of SwiftUI techniques
- **Pro Swift** — a deep-dive into advanced Swift patterns, released in 2016 and updated for free ever since
- **Swift Design Patterns** — covering MVC, MVVM, delegation, protocols, and 22 patterns total
- **Testing Swift** — dedicated coverage of unit and UI testing practices
- **Swift Concurrency by Example** — covering async/await and structured concurrency
- **SwiftData by Example** — covering Apple's new persistence framework

He also created the **Unwrap** app (free, no in-app purchases), an iPhone/iPad learning tool with nearly 100 video lessons teaching Swift fundamentals, interactive reviews, and daily challenges.

His most celebrated product innovations are the **100 Days of Swift** and **100 Days of SwiftUI** — free, structured 100-day learning challenges that require one hour of study per day plus daily public accountability posts. These courses have generated an enormous community of learners worldwide who share progress publicly, mentor each other, and reference Hudson's material as the canonical beginner path.

He co-hosts the **Swift over Coffee** podcast (previously with Erica Sadun, later with Mikaela Caron), covering Swift community news and developer perspectives.

He also runs **Hacking with Swift Live**, an online conference where all profits go to charity — raising $38,000 in 2020 and continuing to donate proceeds to charities such as Julian House in 2023.

In 2024 he released **Ignite**, an open-source static site generator that lets Swift developers build websites entirely in Swift with SwiftUI-like syntax.

In 2025 he launched **Hacktivate**, an app that teaches real-world computer science and security skills through 240 capture-the-flag missions on iPhone, iPad, and Mac.

## Why He Is on This Team

Paul Hudson is the clearest working example of a sustainable solo educational content business built entirely on trust and generosity. His model — give almost everything away for free, offer a premium subscription for those who want more — has allowed him to operate independently for over a decade without venture capital, advertising that compromises integrity, or a corporate structure. He has demonstrated that free content, consistently maintained, can be both the product and the marketing funnel simultaneously.

His voice is essential for any decision involving: educational product design, the free-to-paid content business model, accessibility as a first-class concern in app development, teaching by doing rather than explaining, and building community around structured learning challenges.
