# Paul Hudson — Quotes

Quotes are drawn from verifiable published sources. Where a quote is paraphrased from summaries of inaccessible pages, it is noted as such and not presented as verbatim.

---

> "Programming is an art. Don't spend all your time sharpening your pencil when you should be drawing."

— hackingwithswift.com/about (site bio, undated; consistent across multiple years)

---

> "I know some other folks charge for new editions, but that's something I refuse to do — when you trust me with your learning, I want to go above and beyond what you expect."

— @twostraws on X (Twitter), September 17, 2024, regarding Pro Swift's eighth year of free updates (x.com/twostraws/status/1836045290995884420)

---

> "Accessibility isn't something that's 'nice to have' — it should be regarded as a fundamental part of your application design, and considered from the very beginning onwards."

— hackingwithswift.com/books/ios-swiftui/accessibility-wrap-up (Hacking with iOS: SwiftUI Edition, Accessibility: Wrap up chapter)

---

> "I'm also pleased to announce I'll be updating the 100 Days of SwiftUI for iOS 17 once the final version has shipped. This will move everything over to the latest Swift and SwiftUI, including SwiftData, and will continue to be free for everyone."

— @twostraws on X (Twitter), June 9, 2023 (x.com/twostraws/status/1667194153489539072)

---

> "Go big on SwiftUI in your personal projects and any side projects, because that's where you'll learn the fastest."

— Paul Hudson, WWDC Developer Interview, wwdcbysundell.com/2019/interviews/paul-hudson/, 2019

---

> "I can work on some code to the point where I think it's right, but being able to show the folks who made it and say 'how's this?' is huge."

— Paul Hudson, on attending SwiftUI labs at WWDC, wwdcbysundell.com/2019/interviews/paul-hudson/, 2019

---

> "By default SwiftUI apps come with a remarkably high level of accessibility, which was planned into the framework from the earliest days — unless you actively choose to work around the defaults you'll find your apps do a good job of being accessible without much extra work from you."

— hackingwithswift.com/quick-start/swiftui/introduction-to-accessibility-with-swiftui (SwiftUI by Example: Introduction to accessibility)

---

> "It's hard not to be impressed by SwiftUI. That something so massive could be kept almost completely secret is just incredible!"

— Paul Hudson, WWDC Developer Interview, wwdcbysundell.com/2019/interviews/paul-hudson/, 2019

---

> "The Swift Career Accelerator is the most comprehensive, career-transforming learning resource ever created for iOS development."

— hackingwithswift.com/articles/272/swift-career-accelerator (November 2025 article describing the product)

**Note on fabrication policy:** Several quotes commonly attributed to Paul Hudson in secondary sources — including the paraphrase "you don't learn Swift by reading about Swift" — could not be traced to a verifiable primary source in research for this profile. They are not included here. Only quotes linked to specific URLs, tweet IDs, or published book text are included. If the profile needs additional verifiable quotes, the Hacking with Swift newsletter archive (twostraws.gumroad.com) and his Mastodon profile (mastodon.social/@twostraws) are the most productive sources.
