# Team Alto / Snowman — Quotes

*Note: Only quotes that are directly sourced and attributable are included here. Paraphrased or reconstructed statements appear in other profile files, not here.*

---

> "We wanted to create a piece of art that just happened to be a video game."
— Ryan Cash, Surviving the App Store interview (survivingtheappstore.com), c. 2015

---

> "We wanted to build a casual endless runner...without any of the usual junk."
— Ryan Cash, "The Story Behind Alto's Adventure," ryancash.net, April 2015

---

> "Everything matters. You can make a killer game, but if everything else isn't on par, it's not going to be a success."
— Ryan Cash, 80.lv interview on Alto's Adventure, 2015

---

> "The game's environments ARE the main character, with Alto and the gang being secondary."
— Harry Nesbitt, Road to the IGF: Alto's Odyssey, Game Developer, 2018

---

> "How far can you simplify a form until you're left only with its essence...the player's mind can do all the hard work of filling in the blanks."
— Harry Nesbitt, Road to the IGF: Alto's Odyssey, Game Developer, 2018

---

> "The Alto series is much more about capturing a feeling than it is about recreating a high-fidelity extreme sports experience."
— Eli Cymet (Producer, Team Alto), The Sweet Setup Q&A, 2018

---

> "We would get a lot of letters...telling us that Alto helped people relax at night and cope with illness or loss."
— Eli Cymet (Producer, Team Alto), Engadget interview, February 2018

---

> "If you make something for yourself, there will always be others looking for the same thing."
— Jordan Rosenberg, Snowman 10-Year Anniversary, builtbysnowman.medium.com, April 2022

---

> "I think you'll see that DNA in a lot of the stuff we do. It's about the elegance of motion, getting into a flow and a rhythm…"
— Ryan Cash, mobilesyrup.com Laya's Horizon interview, May 2023

---

> "We like to make games where we don't have to worry about flooding players with ads."
— Ryan Cash, Pocket Gamer Laya's Horizon developer interview, May 2023

---

> "We can design games right from the start without having to worry about ads or purchases."
— Owais Akhtar (Lead Producer, Snowman), Pocket Gamer Laya's Horizon developer interview, May 2023

---

> "I think we'd always err on the side of premium, so we don't have to think about monetization when we're designing a game."
— Ryan Cash, Android Authority interview on Alto's Odyssey, August 2018

---

> "At the heart of the Alto series is an exacting focus on polish, down to the very smallest details."
— Snowman, "Alto's Odyssey Wins An Apple Design Award!," blog.builtbysnowman.com, June 2018

---

> "One of the things I'm interested in as a game designer are controls that are what I call kinesthetic — they feel like something."
— Jason Medeiros (Creative Director, Snowman), mobilesyrup.com Laya's Horizon interview, May 2023

---

> "We are a small studio at the centre of artful experiences. Whether as a developer or creative partner, our goal has always been the same: to create meaningful experiences that you may sit with for a short time but that sit with you for a long while."
— Snowman, About page, builtbysnowman.com (accessed 2024)

---

> "We didn't want to treat a followup as a forgone conclusion, and also didn't want to make an 'Alto's Adventure 2.'"
— Ryan Cash, Android Authority interview on Alto's Odyssey, August 2018

---

> "Learning how to make a good game is not the same as making an app...games are heavily based on feeling."
— Ryan Cash, PocketGamer.biz Indie Spotlight interview, January 2020

---

> "We want to spark the player's imagination, and hopefully leave them feeling curious about what exists beyond the screen."
— Harry Nesbitt, Road to the IGF: Alto's Odyssey, Game Developer, 2018
