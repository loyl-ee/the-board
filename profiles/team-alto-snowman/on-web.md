# Team Alto / Snowman — On Web

## The Website as Product Reflection

Snowman's web presence at builtbysnowman.com reflects the same philosophy as their games: clean, uncluttered, atmospheric, and quietly confident. The homepage identifies them as "A small studio handcrafting big ideas" — a tagline that says everything about scale, intention, and craft without overselling. The site organises their work straightforwardly: games, apps, community, and contact. There is no aggressive marketing copy, no prominent press quote wall, no growth-metric boasting. The web presence is functional and beautiful in the same proportion as their games.

## Marketing Through Atmosphere, Not Features

Snowman's marketing materials — including trailers, press kits, and social posts — are characteristically atmospheric. The trailers for Alto's Adventure and Alto's Odyssey are not feature lists set to music. They are visual poems: time-lapse skies, snowboarding silhouettes, the particular quality of light at each time of day, all cut to the game's original soundtrack. They market by showing what the experience feels like rather than explaining what it does. This is a coherent extension of their design philosophy into their communications strategy. If the game is about creating an emotional impression rather than explaining itself, the marketing should do the same.

## Medium as Transparent Communication

Snowman maintains an active presence on Medium (builtbysnowman.medium.com), which they use for milestone posts, retrospectives, and announcements. These posts — including a 10-year anniversary reflection (2022) and a 5-year retrospective on Skate City and Where Cards Fall — are written in a warm, honest voice that reflects the team's actual thinking rather than corporate messaging. They acknowledge uncertainty, celebrate collaborators generously, and speak to players as partners in the experience rather than as consumers.

## Art and Craft Podcast

Snowman produced a podcast called Art and Craft, described as sharing "stories from diverse and wonderful people in the game industry." Each episode centred on a single person and explored what they had helped create — developers, artists, writers, producers, musicians. The podcast is consistent with Snowman's broader orientation: they are interested in the creative process, in the people behind work, and in the industry as a craft community rather than a business sector.

## Web Presence Gap Note

Snowman does not have a significant presence on the broader web beyond their own channels and press appearances. They do not appear to run content marketing campaigns, SEO-optimised editorial, or paid social advertising in any notable way. Their web strategy is essentially: make excellent products, participate in the platforms that distribute them (App Store, Apple Arcade, Netflix), and let the work speak. This is a viable strategy for a studio with their reputation and platform relationships, but it means discovery is almost entirely platform- and press-dependent.
