# Kevin Rose

## Who He Is

Kevin Rose (born 1977, California) is an American entrepreneur, investor, and media personality whose career has tracked the internet's most formative inflection points — from the early web to social media, mobile, crypto, and AI. He dropped out of a computer science program at the University of Nevada Las Vegas in 1998 and found his first foothold at TechTV, co-hosting the tech culture show *The Screen Savers* before striking out to build companies of his own.

He is best known as the founder of Digg, the social news platform he launched in December 2004 with Owen Byrne, Ron Gorodetzky, and Jay Adelson. Digg pioneered the concept of community-driven content ranking — ordinary users voting stories up or down to determine what reached the homepage — years before the term "social media" became ubiquitous. At its peak, Digg attracted over 38 million monthly users and Rose became a symbol of Web 2.0 entrepreneurship, appearing on the cover of BusinessWeek and named to MIT Technology Review's TR35 list of innovators under 35.

## Career Arc

After Digg, Rose co-founded Revision3 (an early internet television network, acquired by Discovery Communications for $30M+ in 2012), Pownce (a micro-blogging platform acquired by Six Apart in 2008), and Milk (a mobile app studio that Google acquired in 2012). He spent 2012–2015 at Google Ventures as a General Partner, backing companies including Uber, Slack, Blue Bottle Coffee, Nextdoor, Medium, and Ripple.

He then became CEO of Hodinkee, the prestige watch media and e-commerce brand, growing the company substantially before departing to join True Ventures as a Partner in 2017. At True Ventures, he focuses on consumer internet, life sciences, and AI investments.

In 2017, Rose also launched two wellness apps: **Oak** (a free meditation app built around minimal design and traditional technique) and **Zero** (an intermittent fasting tracker, later sold to Big Sky Health). Both reflected his deepening interest in the intersection of technology and personal health practices.

From 2021–2023, Rose co-founded PROOF Collective and launched the Moonbirds NFT project, generating $280 million in primary and secondary sales within its first two days. PROOF was acquired by Yuga Labs in 2023. In 2025, he re-purchased Digg alongside Reddit co-founder Alexis Ohanian and designer Justin Mezzell, relaunching it as a human-first alternative to algorithmically-degraded social feeds, with a public beta opening January 14, 2026.

## Why He Is on This Team

Rose occupies a unique position on the board: he is neither a pure technologist nor a pure investor, but a builder-investor who has personally created products across multiple technology waves and lived through the consequences — including high-profile failures. His experience with Digg's community collapse, the NFT bear market, and the hard lessons of scaling Hodinkee give him a hard-won skepticism that complements his enthusiasm for new technology. He is an essential voice for decisions involving community products, habit-forming apps, wellness technology, and early-stage consumer bets. He is a Sanbo Zen practitioner, Japanese woodworking enthusiast, and co-host of *The Random Show* with Tim Ferriss — an ongoing record of his evolving thinking at the edge of technology and human practice.
