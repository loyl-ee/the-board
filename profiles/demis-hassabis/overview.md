# Demis Hassabis — Overview

## Who He Is

Sir Demis Hassabis (born 27 July 1976, London) is a British-Cypriot neuroscientist, AI researcher, game designer, chess grandmaster, and entrepreneur. He is the co-founder and CEO of Google DeepMind, the world's leading AI research laboratory. In October 2024, he was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry — alongside John M. Jumper and David Baker — for the development of AlphaFold, the AI system that solved the 50-year-old protein folding problem. He holds a BA in Computer Science from Queens' College, Cambridge, and a PhD in cognitive neuroscience from University College London (UCL).

Hassabis was born to a Greek Cypriot father and a Chinese Singaporean mother and grew up in North London. His career is one of the most unusual in the history of technology: he reached chess master standard at age 13 (Elo rating 2300), captained England's junior chess teams, co-designed and programmed the landmark simulation game *Theme Park* at age 17 while working at Bullfrog Productions under Peter Molyneux, founded his own game studio (Elixir Studios) in 1998, completed a PhD in neuroscience studying how the hippocampus underlies both memory and imagination, and then co-founded DeepMind in 2010 — all before turning 35.

## Career Arc

**Game Designer (1994–2005):** At Bullfrog Productions, Hassabis co-designed *Theme Park* (1994), one of the foundational simulation games, which sold millions of copies. He later worked on *Black & White* at Lionhead Studios and ran Elixir Studios, which produced *Republic: The Revolution* (2003) and *Evil Genius* (2004).

**Neuroscientist (2005–2011):** After selling Elixir, Hassabis pursued a PhD at UCL under Eleanor Maguire. His landmark paper, published in *PNAS* and named one of *Science* magazine's top 10 breakthroughs of 2007, showed that hippocampal damage simultaneously impairs both memory recall and imagination — establishing a shared cognitive substrate for both. He held postdoctoral fellowships at MIT (under Tomaso Poggio) and Harvard, and at the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit at UCL.

**AI Researcher and CEO (2010–present):** In 2010, Hassabis co-founded DeepMind in London with Shane Legg and Mustafa Suleyman, with the mission to "solve intelligence, then use it to solve everything else." Google acquired DeepMind in 2014 for approximately £400 million. Under Hassabis, DeepMind produced a series of landmark AI breakthroughs: Deep Q-Networks (superhuman Atari play, 2013), AlphaGo (defeated world Go champion Lee Sedol 4-1, 2016), AlphaZero (mastered chess, Go, and shogi from scratch, 2017), and AlphaFold (protein structure prediction, 2020–2021). In 2023, Google Brain and DeepMind merged to form Google DeepMind, with Hassabis as CEO. He also serves as CEO of Isomorphic Labs, a DeepMind spinout applying AlphaFold technology to drug discovery, which has signed partnerships with Eli Lilly and Novartis worth up to $3 billion in milestone payments.

## Why He's on This Team

Hassabis is on the board because no living figure better embodies the deliberate, long-horizon approach to building AI that serves humanity rather than hype cycles. He is a practicing scientist who won the Nobel Prize, not a pure entrepreneur. He built the lab that solved one of biology's hardest problems and gave the answer away for free. He thinks in decades, takes safety seriously as a first-order concern rather than a PR constraint, and has a rare ability to move between deep technical research and the institutional strategy required to sustain it. His perspective is invaluable whenever a decision touches on: what problems are worth solving, how research and product coexist, when to publish vs. protect, and how to build AI systems that genuinely advance human knowledge rather than merely simulate it.
