# Demis Hassabis — Quotes

A collection of verified verbatim quotes from primary sources. All quotes are attributed to named interviews, lectures, or documented publications.

---

> "Step one, solve intelligence; step two, use it to solve everything else."

— DeepMind founding mission statement, cited in The Atlantic (sponsored content); frequently attributed to Hassabis in interviews.

---

> "I'm passionate about science and finding out knowledge... If we could build AI in the right way, it could be the ultimate tool to help scientists. I hope AlphaFold is a first example of that."

— Nobel Prize in Chemistry interview, NobelPrize.org, October 2024.

---

> "We released all these 3D predictions for the benefit of the scientific community and actually as a sort of gift to humanity."

— TED Interview / DeepMind lecture, describing the AlphaFold database release.

---

> "These systems, they're tools. They're very good at analyzing data... They can't figure out what the right question is to ask. The best scientists paired with these tools will be able to do incredible things."

— Nobel Prize in Chemistry interview, NobelPrize.org, October 2024.

---

> "I've worked my whole life on AI because I believe in its incredible potential to advance science & medicine, and improve billions of people's lives. But as with any transformative technology we should apply the precautionary principle, and build & deploy it with exceptional care."

— X (formerly Twitter), @demishassabis, May 2023.

---

> "I don't have a p(doom) number because I think it would imply a level of precision that is not there. It's definitely non-zero and it's probably non-negligible. So that in itself is pretty sobering."

— Interview reported by machine.news, cited across multiple outlets.

---

> "At the back of my mind, I've got this gnawing feeling that there's something much more important, much bigger than the commercial race, which is getting AGI safely over the line for humanity and to make sure that the benefits fully outweigh the risks."

— Fortune, April 2026.

---

> "The people that are making artificial intelligence shouldn't just be from 20 square miles of the U.S."

— Fortune, April 2026.

---

> "I think some of the biggest problems that face us today as a society, whether that's climate or disease, will be helped by AI solutions. I'd be very worried about society today if I didn't know that something as transformative as AI was coming down the line."

— Time Magazine, April 2025.

---

> "It is in this collaboration between people and algorithms that incredible scientific progress lies over the next few decades."

— Financial Times, 2017.

---

> "This technology has such potential for enormous, enormous good, but it's a dual-use technology. So if bad actors get hold of it, it could be used for bad things."

— New York Times, 2023.

---

> "For the first time in human history we wouldn't be resource constrained. And I think that could be amazing new era for humanity where it's not zero-sum."

— Lex Fridman Podcast #475, July 2025.

---

> "We did a billion years of PhD time in one year."

— Various interviews, describing AlphaFold's prediction of 200 million protein structures.

---

> "In ten, 15 years... we'll look back on medicine we have today... like medieval times."

— Possible podcast interview with Demis Hassabis, source documented on possible.fm.

---

> "I think there are valid concerns and they should be discussed and debated now, decades before there's anything that's actually of any potential consequence or power that we need to worry about, so we have the answers in place well ahead of time."

— BBC, 2015.

---

> "I want to understand the big questions, the really big ones that you normally go into philosophy or physics if you're interested in. I thought building AI would be the fastest route to answer some of those questions."

— Time Magazine, 2023.
