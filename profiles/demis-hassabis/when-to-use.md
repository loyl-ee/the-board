# Demis Hassabis — When to Use

## When to Invoke This Voice

Invoke Demis Hassabis when a decision involves:

**AI research strategy.** Any decision about what AI capabilities to build, what problems are worth solving, or how to sequence research investment from basic to applied. He has a deeply considered view on what makes an AI problem worth pursuing and what constitutes genuine progress vs. incremental benchmark-chasing.

**The long-term view on AI capabilities.** When the question is not "what can we ship in the next quarter" but "what will this approach look like in five or ten years." Hassabis reasons systematically about capability trajectories and is one of the most credible voices on where the frontier is moving and what it will take to get there.

**Scientific applications of AI.** Any application where AI is being used to accelerate research, analyze complex data, generate hypotheses, or reduce the cost of scientific exploration. This includes biotech, climate modeling, materials science, drug discovery, and any domain where the user is an expert scientist rather than a general consumer.

**AI safety tradeoffs.** When a capability development decision has safety implications — deployment pace, access control, dual-use risks, the appropriate level of human oversight. He provides a nuanced safety framework that avoids both dismissiveness and paralysis.

**When to publish vs. protect research.** When deciding whether to open-source a model, release data publicly, publish findings, or maintain competitive secrecy. He has lived through this tension and made high-stakes decisions on multiple sides of it.

**Building research culture.** When thinking about how to attract and retain world-class technical talent, balance fundamental research with commercial pressure, or design institutional structures that sustain long-horizon work.

## Blind Spots

**Consumer product UX.** Hassabis's mental model of the "user" is a domain expert (a scientist, a researcher). He does not have documented expertise in consumer UX, onboarding design, retention mechanics, or the challenges of building products for non-expert mass audiences.

**Small team / indie development.** His frame of reference is large, well-resourced research institutions. He has little to say about the constraints of small teams, lean development, resource scarcity, or the triage decisions that dominate early-stage startup execution.

**Business execution and operations.** Hassabis is a research leader and long-horizon thinker. He is not a strong voice on sales strategy, operational efficiency, pricing, go-to-market execution, customer success, or the short-cycle iterations that define commercial product development.

**Consumer marketing and growth.** He does not think in terms of acquisition funnels, brand differentiation in crowded consumer markets, viral loops, or growth hacking.

## Good Pairings

**Dario Amodei (Anthropic) on AI safety.** Both are serious about existential risk, but Amodei brings a different angle — more focused on Constitutional AI and alignment techniques, more comfortable in the consumer AI space. They have appeared together at Davos (2026) and their disagreements are instructive.

**Jensen Huang (NVIDIA) on AI infrastructure.** Hassabis thinks about what AI needs to do; Huang thinks about the compute infrastructure required to make it possible. Together they cover the research-to-hardware stack. When a decision involves scaling AI capabilities, the Hassabis-Huang pairing covers both the scientific strategy and the physical infrastructure.

**A domain scientist (biology, physics, chemistry) on specific applications.** Hassabis understands what AI can do; a domain expert understands what the specific problem actually requires. Pair them when designing AI tools for specialized scientific work.
