# Lucas Pope — On Web

## Web Presence and Distribution

Lucas Pope operates primarily through Steam for PC and macOS distribution, which accounts for the bulk of his commercial revenue. His smaller and experimental works — including *Moida Mansion* (2024) and earlier jam games — are available for free on itch.io, where he operates as **dukope** (dukope.itch.io). He has used itch.io as a platform for low-stakes releases: experiments, jam games, and work that sits outside his main commercial catalog.

His personal site, dukope.com, serves as a catalog of his work rather than an active content hub. He maintains social media presence on X (formerly Twitter) as @dukope, and has a Mastodon account at mastodon.social/@dukope.

## itch.io as a Distribution Channel

Pope's use of itch.io reflects the platform's role in indie game culture: a space for smaller, experimental, or free work that does not fit the commercial Steam pipeline. His itch.io catalog includes experimental titles and jam games that he releases without commercial ambition. The platform also served as an early distribution point for the *Return of the Obra Dinn* demo in 2016, which he released publicly to gather player feedback before the full release in 2018.

## Views on Platform Distribution

Pope has not made extensive public statements about platform strategy or digital distribution philosophy. What is documented is that his major commercial releases (*Papers, Please*, *Return of the Obra Dinn*) launched on PC/Mac first, with console ports following later — suggesting a PC-first posture consistent with his solo development workflow and the technical constraints of his tools (Unity, Haxe, custom pipelines).

*Mars After Midnight* (2024) was released exclusively for the Playdate handheld, demonstrating willingness to commit to a platform-exclusive release when the platform's constraints genuinely inspire the work.

## Honest Gap Note

Lucas Pope has not published detailed public statements about his views on web-based game distribution, browser games as a platform, or the economics of itch.io versus Steam. His known distribution decisions are pragmatic rather than ideological: commercial work goes to Steam (the largest PC marketplace), smaller work goes to itch.io, and experimental platform commitments (Playdate) happen when the hardware inspires the idea. Direct statements comparing platforms or articulating a distribution philosophy are not available in his documented interviews.
