# Lucas Pope — Quotes

Verbatim quotes with verified sources. All quotes are drawn from documented interviews, press releases, or published accounts. No quotes are fabricated or paraphrased.

---

> "Don't tell or show something when you can make the player do it themselves instead. For me, this is the power that interactive games have over other media. You can put the player in exactly the situation you want to share and their connection with the message will be more powerful."

— Reason magazine interview, September 2013

---

> "Because games are interactive, I think the potential to affect others will be much stronger with games than with other media."

— Reason magazine interview, September 2013

---

> "I developed the game from the bottom up. Only once I had the core mechanics of document checking working well did I start to think about a narrative."

— Reason magazine interview, September 2013

---

> "I wasn't expecting this response at all. Papers, Please was intended as a small experimental project that I'd finish off before working on something more marketable."

— Reason magazine interview, September 2013

---

> "If there's no problem, then I'm not that interested. But if there's a problem, if there's some restriction or some limitation, then I'm interested suddenly."

— Game Developer / Gamasutra, on Return of the Obra Dinn design problems, 2018

---

> "I chose the aesthetic because it stood out, but I also chose it because it's a good style if you want to obscure things, if you want to leave stuff out. The lighting is very sharp, and it leaves a lot of detail out."

— PC Gamer, on Return of the Obra Dinn's 1-bit aesthetic

---

> "I wanted to make a 1-bit, 3D game, because I grew up playing Mac Plus games. That 1-bit style — high-contrast, what passed as 'high-resolution' back then — was a technical limitation back then, of course, but it really stuck with me over the years."

— PC Gamer, on the origin of Return of the Obra Dinn's visual style

---

> "My goal for Obra Dinn was to overwhelm the player initially, then give them time to get comfortable with the core loop before relying on their internal compulsions to motivate them."

— Game Developer / Gamasutra, Road to the IGF: Return of the Obra Dinn

---

> "It blows my mind that it's only becoming more and more relevant over time. Honestly, it's a tragedy. You want your work to be relevant, but at the same time, wow, I really wish it was not that fucking relevant."

— PC Gamer interview, on Papers, Please's contemporary relevance, 2025

---

> "I'm okay wasting my own time and energy with experimentation but would feel quite bad about inflicting that on someone else."

— PreMortem Games interview, October 2022

---

> "make it different enough from other games that if you want this kind of game you pretty much just gotta buy this one game."

— Game Developer, on his marketing philosophy for Return of the Obra Dinn

---

> "It sounds bad that I have to do everything myself but it also means there's no skull barrier between the ideas."

— TechRadar interview, March 2024

---

> "Ideas are easy but good ideas that keep me awake at night are the ones I'm after."

— PreMortem Games interview, October 2022

---

> "Games are the perfect combination of everything I enjoy."

— PreMortem Games interview, October 2022

---

> "I basically jumped in on the visuals and sorted out the rest over four and a half years of development."

— Game Developer, Road to the IGF: Return of the Obra Dinn
