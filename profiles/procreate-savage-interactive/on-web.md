# Procreate & Savage Interactive — On Web

## Web Presence Overview

Savage Interactive's primary web domain is procreate.com (previously procreate.art). The site is clean and product-focused, organized around the three products — Procreate, Procreate Pocket, and Procreate Dreams — along with an Insight section (blog/announcements), a Folio community hub, a Careers page, and support documentation accessed through the Procreate Handbook at help.procreate.com.

The site's aesthetic mirrors the product: dark background, maximum whitespace, minimal navigation chrome, artwork-forward. The visual language communicates that this is a tool for artists, designed by people who understand visual craft. There are no growth-hacking CTAs, no email capture pop-ups, and no subscription upsells. The call to action is "Download on the App Store." The site exists to represent the product honestly, not to convert through pressure.

## The Insight Section

Procreate's Insight section functions as both announcement platform and editorial voice. Major product releases, feature updates, and significant company statements (including the August 2024 generative AI stance) are published here. The writing is direct and specific — not marketing copy. The Insight posts read as genuine communication from the team to the user community rather than as press releases. The generative AI post, for example, was a short, unhedged statement of position that spread virally precisely because it did not read like a corporate document.

## YouTube Channel

The official Procreate YouTube channel is organized into structured series: Beginner tutorials, feature deep-dives, Live Draw sessions with professional artists, and the Procreate Asks Artists interview series. The channel serves as the primary education platform and functions independently of any paid advertising or promotional framing. The implicit marketing effect — showing professionals creating extraordinary work in Procreate — is more powerful than explicit promotion would be.

## Social Media

Procreate maintains an Instagram account (3 million+ followers) and Twitter/X presence. The social accounts are artist-forward: artwork from the community, feature announcement videos, and occasional editorial statements (of which the generative AI post is the most notable example). Social media for Procreate is a community showcase rather than a brand channel — the product markets itself through the work made with it.

**Gap note:** Detailed analytics on web traffic, YouTube subscriber counts, or broader digital strategy documentation are not publicly available.
