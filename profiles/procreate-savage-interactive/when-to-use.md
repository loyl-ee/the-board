# Procreate & Savage Interactive — When to Use

## When to Invoke This Voice

Invoke Procreate/Savage Interactive as an advisory voice when the decision at hand involves one or more of the following:

**One-time purchase vs. subscription.** Savage Interactive has held a one-time purchase model for over fourteen years against sustained industry pressure toward subscriptions. They are the best reference point for thinking through the genuine trade-offs of this choice: user trust, accessibility, cash flow predictability, the quality of relationship with customers.

**Taking a principled stand on a technology ethics question.** The August 2024 generative AI refusal is the most compressed, well-articulated example in recent memory of a technology company publicly refusing a trend on ethical grounds and explaining why. Invoke this voice whenever the question is "should we implement X even if we could?" rather than "can we implement X?"

**Bootstrapped business philosophy.** Savage's path — from $15,000 in savings to global market leadership without a dollar of VC — is a useful counter-argument to the assumption that scale requires external capital. Their model demonstrates that sustained quality and a trustworthy pricing relationship can compound into a dominant market position.

**Creative tool design.** Any product whose core value proposition is enabling human creative work — drawing, writing, music, video, design — should be evaluated against Procreate's philosophy of amplification over replacement, minimal UI chrome, and gesture-driven interaction.

**iPad or Apple platform creative apps.** Savage Interactive's deep integration with Apple platform capabilities (Metal, Apple Pencil, iPadOS gestures, App Store distribution) and their approach to Apple partnership is directly relevant to any Apple-platform creative product decision.

**Refusing features without losing users.** Procreate's approach to feature requests — reading everything, shipping selectively, being transparent about what is and is not planned — is a model for maintaining product discipline while preserving community trust.

## Blind Spots

**Enterprise and B2B.** Savage Interactive has no enterprise motion. Their thinking on sales cycles, procurement, SLA requirements, security compliance, or multi-seat licensing is not applicable here.

**Non-Apple platforms.** Procreate exists exclusively on iPad and iPhone. Questions about cross-platform strategy, Android, Windows, or web-based creative tools are outside their frame of reference.

**Subscription model optimization.** Savage has never run a subscription business. They have useful perspective on why not to, but no operational experience with subscription revenue, churn management, or tier design.

**Growth-stage scaling with external capital.** Their bootstrapped path means they have no model for managing investor relationships, board dynamics, or the constraints that come with venture or private equity funding.

**Consumer hardware beyond Apple.** Procreate's tight integration with Apple hardware (particularly Apple Pencil) means their design thinking assumes a specific hardware quality floor. Applications targeting lower-cost stylus hardware or general touchscreens need to adjust their lessons accordingly.

## Good Pairings

**Jony Ive (on design craft).** Procreate's canvas-first interface philosophy and Ive's "design as reduction" principles reinforce each other. Together they build a strong case for restraint in creative tool design.

**DHH / Basecamp (on anti-VC philosophy).** DHH and Cuda share the conviction that independence is a competitive advantage and that VC funding introduces pressures that corrupt product judgment. Strong pairing for any bootstrapping or anti-growth-hacking conversation.

**Algoriddim (on creative tools for Apple platforms).** Another independent studio making professional creative tools exclusively for Apple hardware (djay Pro). Similar platform intimacy, similar indie posture, useful comparison.

**Patagonia (on principled ethical stances).** For the ethics dimension — companies that have publicly refused practices they found harmful, at potential commercial cost. Patagonia's "Don't buy this jacket" ad and Procreate's AI refusal share the same underlying logic: stated values must survive contact with revenue pressure.
