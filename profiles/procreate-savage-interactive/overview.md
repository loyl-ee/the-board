# Procreate & Savage Interactive — Overview

## Who They Are

Savage Interactive is an independent software studio based in Hobart, Tasmania, Australia. Founded in 2010 by James Cuda and his wife Alanna — on the day Apple officially announced the iPad — the company exists for a single, focused purpose: to build the best creative tools for Apple's touch platforms. Their flagship product, Procreate, launched on March 8, 2011, and has grown into the most widely used paid drawing application on iPad, held that top position on the App Store since 2018, and accumulated nearly 30 million downloads globally.

The studio's origin is a genuine bootstrapper's story. Cuda, an artist who had taught himself to code and built a Tasmanian design agency (Savage Media) with Alanna, witnessed Steve Jobs unveil the iPad in 2010 and immediately recognized what the device could mean for artists. He shut down the agency, partnered with self-taught developer Lloyd Bottomley as co-founder, and the two worked nights and weekends through more than 100 design reviews and three complete rebuilds, launching Procreate with just $15,000 in savings.

## The Products

**Procreate** (iPad, 2011–present) is a raster graphics and digital painting application built on Savage's proprietary Valkyrie graphics engine, which runs atop Apple's Metal framework. It offers a full professional toolset — layers, masks, blending modes, over 190 brushes across 18 categories, full Apple Pencil integration — while deliberately keeping its interface minimal and gesture-driven.

**Procreate Pocket** (iPhone, 2014) brings the core Procreate experience to iPhone. It won Apple's App of the Year award in December 2018.

**Procreate Dreams** (iPad, November 2023) is Savage's 2D animation platform — a five-year development effort built to make animation accessible without sacrificing professional depth. Procreate Dreams 2, released in December 2025 as a free update, expanded the tool significantly with advanced export, unlimited flipbook tracks, and 180 new brushes.

## Scale and Recognition

Savage Interactive has won three Apple Design Awards: Outstanding Design (2013), Inclusivity (2022), and Innovation (2024, for Procreate Dreams). Procreate is used by professional concept artists at Disney Animation, Pixar, Marvel Comics, and DC Comics. It has appeared in marketing campaigns for films including *Stranger Things*, *Logan*, and *Blade Runner 2049*, and has graced multiple covers of *The New Yorker*. Doug Chiang, the veteran Star Wars concept artist, uses it on productions.

The company operates with roughly 60 employees (team size confirmed by James Cuda in Apple Developer interviews), all based in Hobart. Revenue has been estimated at $32 million EBITDA based on Australian financial reporting; the business has never taken a dollar of venture capital, and the only investors are James and Alanna Cuda. All products are sold as one-time purchases — Procreate for $12.99, Procreate Dreams for $19.99, with all major updates delivered free.

## Why They Are on This Team

Procreate and Savage Interactive represent a specific, rare achievement: global product dominance built from a small team in a remote location, entirely on the studio's own financial terms, through sustained quality and deep respect for the people using the product. Their August 2024 declaration — "We will never implement generative AI. Creativity is made, not generated." — crystallizes a principle relevant to any founder facing pressure to adopt a technology trend that conflicts with their core values. They are on this advisory team as the exemplar of what principled independence can produce.
