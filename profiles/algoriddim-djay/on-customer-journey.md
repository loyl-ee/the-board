# Algoriddim — On Customer Journey

## A Broad Tent with a Narrow Core

djay's 50 million downloads represent an unusually wide user spectrum: casual party-mode users mixing playlists at home, bedroom DJs learning the craft, intermediate hobbyists who perform at small events, and professional DJs who use djay alongside or instead of hardware like Pioneer CDJ setups. Algoriddim has navigated this breadth by ensuring each tier finds genuine value, rather than making the professional tier feel premium and the beginner tier feel patronized.

The free version is the entry point for most users. It is a fully functional DJ tool — not a demo, not a time-limited trial. This means beginners get a real experience and form a genuine relationship with the product before any purchase decision. When they encounter the Pro paywall for Neural Mix or advanced Automix features, the upgrade is a choice to go deeper, not a tax on basic functionality.

## The DJ Community as a User Base

The professional DJ community is small, vocal, and influential. A professional DJ using djay at a club set is an endorsement visible to hundreds of people who see the iPad or iPhone on the decks. Algoriddim has cultivated this community partly through hardware integration (supporting the controllers professional DJs already own) and partly through ambassador relationships — World Champion DJ Eskei83 performed the first VR DJ set using djay for Meta Quest; DJ Angelo has created tutorial content for the platform; DJ Jazzy Jeff has described Neural Mix Pro as "absolutely a game changer."

These endorsements are significant in a category where credibility with professional users is the primary signal of seriousness. A beginner choosing DJ software looks to what professionals trust.

## App Store Presence and Ratings

djay maintains high App Store ratings across markets, a reflection of Algoriddim's consistent quality commitment and responsive update cadence. The company has iterated through every major iOS version without significant regressions — an operational discipline that beginner users may not notice but professional users (who cannot afford tools that break at OS update time) rely on.

## Tutorial and Educational Content

Algoriddim maintains two YouTube channels: an official channel (Algoriddim Official) and a dedicated support channel (Algoriddim Support). The support channel produces structured tutorials covering setup, beatmatching, hardware integration, and Neural Mix usage. Community creators have produced extensive third-party tutorial content in multiple languages — a sign of an engaged user base that generates its own onboarding material.

The app's Automix feature functions as an embedded tutorial mechanism: new users can observe the AI make professional-quality transitions and reverse-engineer the technique. This in-app learning loop reduces dependence on external educational resources and shortens the time from "downloaded the app" to "performed a real mix."

## Platform Migration Support

When Spotify's integration ended in 2020, Algoriddim published explicit migration guides helping users move their playlists to TIDAL and SoundCloud. This response — treating the platform disruption as a user support problem rather than a business problem — reflected attention to the user relationship as distinct from the platform relationship. Users who might have abandoned djay when their Spotify library became inaccessible were instead given a clear path to continue.
