# Algoriddim — Quotes

## Notes on Source Availability

Direct, verbatim quotes from Karim Morsy are moderately available through a small set of primary sources: the Apple Developer feature on djay for visionOS (2024), the zplane interview blog, the Apple App Store story "How Karim makes the world dance," and the iMore interview on M1. Extended interview transcripts are sparse — Morsy is not a frequent media personality, and the Details Matter interview (which appeared to be the most substantial recent source) returned a 404 at time of research. Quotes below are drawn from verifiable sources; sources are cited.

---

> "When we heard about Apple Vision Pro, it felt like djay was this beast that wanted to be unleashed. Our vision — no pun intended — with Algoriddim was to make DJing accessible to everyone."

— Karim Morsy, Apple Developer, "Realizing their vision: How djay designed for visionOS," 2024

---

> "We realized that with a decade of building DJ interfaces, we were taking a lot for granted. So the first chunk of designing for Apple Vision Pro was going back to the drawing board and saying, 'OK, maybe this made sense 10 years ago with a computer and mouse, but why do we need it now? Why should people have to push a button to match tempos — shouldn't that be seamless?'"

— Karim Morsy, Apple Developer, "Realizing their vision: How djay designed for visionOS," 2024

---

> "That piano has had the same interface for hundreds of years. That's what we're trying to reach, that sweet spot between complexity and ease of use. With djay on Vision Pro, it's less about, 'Let's give people bells and whistles,' and more, 'Let's let them have this experience.'"

— Karim Morsy, Apple Developer, "Realizing their vision: How djay designed for visionOS," 2024

---

> "You want to get into a loop where the environment influences you and vice versa."

— Karim Morsy, Apple Developer, "Realizing their vision: How djay designed for visionOS," 2024

---

> "It's a fundamental breakthrough indeed, and the Apple Neural Engine has already been available to developers since the introduction of the iPhone Xs."

— Karim Morsy, iMore, "Interview: Algoriddim CEO Karim Morsy hails 'fundamental breakthrough' of M1," 2021

---

> "AI is going to be really important because it opens up possibilities similar to how digital audio did 20-30 years ago."

— Karim Morsy, zplane interview, "Algoriddim's CEO on Building a Leading DJ Tech Platform," approx. 2020–2021

---

> "Licensing certain components from zplane has allowed us to focus on our strengths, which worked out great."

— Karim Morsy, zplane interview, "Algoriddim's CEO on Building a Leading DJ Tech Platform," approx. 2020–2021

---

> "DJing allows me to share my passion for music with people, to respond to them and to observe how music changes them – that's what has always fascinated me."

— Karim Morsy, Apple App Store Story, "How Karim makes the world dance," approx. 2020

---

> "We didn't have a business, we didn't have a company – we just had a big dream to win an Apple Design Award."

— Karim Morsy, Apple App Store Story, "How Karim makes the world dance," approx. 2020

---

> "We wanted a product that I could use in the club without dragging boxes there, but that my fellow students could also use for their DJ sessions."

— Karim Morsy, Apple App Store Story, "How Karim makes the world dance," approx. 2020

---

> "Collaborating with AudioShake has been an absolute pleasure. Combining our unique expertise enabled us to develop exclusive new DJ technologies."

— Karim Morsy, AudioShake case study / djay Pro 5 launch materials, 2023
