# Algoriddim GmbH & djay — Overview

## Who They Are

Algoriddim GmbH is a Munich-based software company founded in 2006 by Karim Morsy and two university friends. Morsy — who holds degrees in computer science, music, and psychology, and had previously worked at Apple developing iMovie features — set out to create a DJ tool that eliminated the friction between a musician's vision and the technology needed to realize it. The company is headquartered at Königinstr. 33, Munich, Germany, and has remained 100% self-funded and independent since its inception.

Their flagship product, djay, began life as freeware in June 2006 and launched commercially in November 2007 as boxed software for Mac. It was one of the earliest professional DJ applications available, and Algoriddim moved decisively when the iPhone and iPad opened new form factors: djay appeared on iPad in December 2010 and on iPhone in March 2011. Each platform launch was not a port but a rethinking of what DJ software could mean on that device.

## Scale and Recognition

djay has accumulated over 50 million downloads across its various versions and platforms. It is available on iOS, iPadOS, macOS, Windows, Android, visionOS (Apple Vision Pro), and Meta Quest — making it one of the most widely distributed DJ applications in history. Coverage has appeared in The New York Times, The Wall Street Journal, Forbes, Wired, TechCrunch, and The Verge.

Algoriddim has won three Apple Design Awards:

- **2011** — djay for iPad
- **2016** — djay Pro for Mac and iPad
- **2024** — djay for visionOS (Spatial Computing category)

They also won the Google Play Best App award in 2015 and the Microsoft Store Best AI App award in 2024, confirming reach across every major platform ecosystem.

## What Made Them Famous

Algoriddim holds several meaningful firsts: first professional DJ app on iPhone, first DJ software to integrate Spotify for streaming (2014), and first DJ software to implement real-time AI stem separation for live performance (Neural Mix, introduced with djay Pro AI). Each of these firsts was built around a conviction that professional creative tools should be accessible to anyone, not locked behind specialist hardware or technical gatekeeping.

Their Neural Mix technology — developed in collaboration with AI audio company AudioShake — allows DJs to isolate vocals, drums, and instruments from any track in real time, directly on-device, using Apple's Bionic chip and Neural Engine. This was a genuine technical breakthrough: fitting large AI models onto mobile hardware with no meaningful latency, for live performance use.

## Why They're on This Team

Algoriddim represents a specific and rare archetype: a small, independent European studio that has stayed ahead of the technology curve for nearly two decades without raising outside capital or growing into a large corporation. They demonstrate that deep craft, genuine platform integration, and a clear mission ("eliminate the boundaries between consumer and professional-level software") can compound into category leadership. Their handling of AI — as an amplifier of what DJs can do rather than a replacement for the DJ — is a model worth studying for any team building AI features into creative tools.
