# Pieter Levels (levelsio) — Overview

## Who He Is

Pieter Levels (born 1985, Rotterdam, Netherlands) is a Dutch self-taught programmer, serial indie maker, and the most prominent figure in the "build in public" movement. He operates entirely solo — no co-founders, no VC money, no employees beyond occasional contractors — and has built a portfolio of internet products generating over $3 million in annual recurring revenue as of 2024–2025.

He is known online almost exclusively as **@levelsio**, the handle he uses on X (Twitter), where he has amassed over 600,000 followers and conducts much of his product development, customer research, and revenue transparency in real time.

## Background and Origin Story

Levels graduated with a Master's degree in Business and Entrepreneurship from Erasmus University Rotterdam in 2012. Feeling claustrophobic within conventional career tracks, he sold nearly all of his possessions in 2013 and flew to Southeast Asia with a laptop and a backpack. This was not a vacation — it was a permanent lifestyle pivot. He has lived as a digital nomad, moving between over 40 countries and 150 cities, ever since.

After returning briefly to the Netherlands and experiencing a period of depression and anxiety, he publicly committed in March 2014 to launch **12 startups in 12 months** — a radical act of accountability that would define his career. Most of the 12 failed. One, Nomad List (launched July 2014 as his 7th startup), caught fire.

## His Products

- **Nomad List** (2014): A city-ranking platform for digital nomads, comparing cost of living, internet speed, safety, and weather across 1,250+ cities. Started as a crowdsourced spreadsheet, became a $300K–$400K/year product with tens of thousands of paying members.
- **Remote OK** (2015): A remote job board that became one of the most visited remote job sites on the internet (~1M monthly visits). Revenue: $40K–$90K/month. Runs as a single PHP file.
- **Hoodmaps** (2017): Crowdsourced neighborhood maps. A side project / viral experiment.
- **Rebase** (2021): Immigration-as-a-service for remote workers seeking residency abroad.
- **InteriorAI** (2022): AI-powered interior design generator. Hit $10K in its first week; grew to ~$40–50K/month.
- **PhotoAI** (2023): AI photo shoot generator. Went from $0 to $87K MRR ($1M ARR) in 17 days of a viral launch. Reached $132K+ MRR within 18 months.
- **Pieter.com Flight Simulator** (2025): A browser-based flight game built in 3 hours using Cursor AI, generating $38K/month within 10 days of launch.

## The $1M ARR Solo Story

Levels reached seven-figure annual revenue without a team, without venture capital, and without a formal marketing budget. His customer acquisition cost is effectively zero — he generates traffic through organic search (SEO), viral Twitter posts, Product Hunt launches, and press coverage attracted by the novelty of his transparency. By 2024–2025, his combined portfolio was generating over $420K/month at roughly 80% profit margins.

## Why He Replaced Nathan Broadbent on This Board

Levels replaced Nathan Broadbent as the board's voice for indie hacking because Broadbent, while technically accomplished, has a narrower public footprint. Levels has documented his methods more thoroughly, is more actively building in 2024–2026 (including AI-native products), and speaks to the full lifecycle of a bootstrapped product — from idea to launch to community to revenue — with exceptional clarity and a proven multi-million-dollar track record.
