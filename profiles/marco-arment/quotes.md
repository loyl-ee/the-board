# Marco Arment — Verified Quotes

All quotes below are drawn from documented sources — blog posts at marco.org, published interviews, or widely-cited attributed statements. Quotes from quote aggregator sites (AZQuotes, etc.) are included only where the underlying source context supports the attribution. No quotes have been fabricated or paraphrased into quotation marks.

---

> "Attention to detail can't be (and never is) added later. It's an entire development philosophy, methodology, and culture."

— Marco Arment (widely cited; consistent with his writing on quality and design)

---

> "Making a product better often requires removing features."

— Marco Arment (widely cited; consistent with his Overcast and Instapaper decisions)

---

> "Your personal data isn't my business — it's a liability. I want as little as possible. I don't even log IP addresses anymore."

— Overcast 4.2 release post, marco.org, April 27, 2018

---

> "Big data ruined the web, and I'm not going to help bring it to podcasts. Publishers already get enough from Apple to inform ad rates and make content decisions — they don't need more data from my customers."

— Overcast 4.2 release post, marco.org, April 27, 2018

---

> "Since only about 20% of users have paid to unlock all of the features, it always bugged me that the vast majority of Overcast's users were using a severely limited, pretty mediocre version of the app."

— MacStories interview, October 2015 (regarding the shift to the patronage model in Overcast 2)

---

> "I'm never going to win over everybody no matter which features I add, but I do run the risk of alienating my customers and ruining the app's personality if I add too many features (or the wrong features)."

— MacStories interview, October 2015

---

> "The software quality has fallen so much in the last few years that I'm deeply concerned for its future."

— "Apple has lost the functional high ground," marco.org, January 4, 2015

---

> "We don't need major OS releases every year. We don't need each OS release to have a huge list of new features. We need our computers, phones, and tablets to work well first so we can enjoy new features released at a healthy, gradual, sustainable pace."

— "Apple has lost the functional high ground," marco.org, January 4, 2015

---

> "Achieving this much success with Peace just doesn't feel good."

— "Just doesn't feel good," marco.org, September 18, 2015 (explaining why he pulled the Peace ad blocker despite it being the #1 paid app)

---

> "I always want to make the best podcast app, and I'll never disrespect your time, attention, or privacy."

— "Ten years of Overcast: A new foundation," marco.org, July 16, 2024

---

> "Most of Overcast's core code was 10 years old, which made it cumbersome or impossible to easily move with the times."

— "Ten years of Overcast: A new foundation," marco.org, July 16, 2024

---

> "If I did my job well, you'll hardly notice it at all. You'll have no idea that your podcasts are being remastered in your pocket."

— "Introducing Voice Boost 2: Remaster your podcasts," marco.org, January 31, 2020

---

> "Tumblr has given my family a strong safety net and given me the freedom to work on whatever I want."

— marco.org, May 20, 2013 (on the Yahoo acquisition of Tumblr)

---

> "Instapaper is much bigger today than I could have predicted in 2008, and it has simply grown far beyond what one person can do."

— "The next generation of Instapaper," marco.org, April 25, 2013 (explaining the sale to Betaworks)

---

> "The biggest mistake I see indies make is having unrealistic expectations of how much the market will value what they're making."

— Kodeco interview (raywenderlich.com), on indie development

---

> "Apple's leaders continue to deny developers of two obvious truths: that our apps provide substantial value to iOS beyond the purchase commissions collected by Apple, and that without our apps, the iPhone has little value to most of its customers today."

— "Developer relations," marco.org, June 3, 2021

---

> "Good accessibility isn't just the right thing to do, but it's also good business, and it makes the app better for everyone."

— Curb Cuts interview, April 2026

---

> "If you sit on, sleep on, stare at, or touch something for more than an hour a day, spend whatever it takes to get the best."

— Marco Arment, Twitter, May 8, 2010 (on quality of everyday objects)
