# Jordan Singer — Quotes

Note: Singer is a moderately documented individual. He has not published a substantial body of long-form essays, and his most characteristic statements appear in tweets, newsletter posts, podcast interviews, and press profiles. The quotes below are drawn from those sources, cited as specifically as documentation allows. Where a quote appeared in an article quoting Singer, that is noted.

---

> "This changes everything. With GPT-3, I built a Figma plugin to design for you. I call it 'Designer'."

— @jsngr on Twitter/X, July 18, 2020 (the tweet that established his reputation in AI design)

---

> "It's not gonna replace you as a designer. Yet."

— Jordan Singer, ibuildmyideas.substack.com newsletter, July 2020 (honest assessment of GPT-3 design generation limits, written in context of building the "Designer" plugin)

---

> "I prefer to have people using the real thing and giving early feedback."

— Jordan Singer, quoted in Mercury/Meridian profile interview (mercury.com/blog/jordan-singer)

---

> "There is nothing like seeing what you're dreaming of to be able to react to it."

— Jordan Singer, "Automating Figma," ibuildmyideas.substack.com

---

> "There is nothing more important than building momentum through shipping and iterating on feedback."

— Jordan Singer, "Automating Figma," ibuildmyideas.substack.com

---

> "Learning to code as a designer is invaluable. Applying a computer science mindset to design ultimately made me a better designer."

— Jordan Singer, Playbook (askplaybook.com/jordan)

---

> "The details that you might think to be overkill can end up being truly meaningful for the product's experience."

— Jordan Singer, Playbook (askplaybook.com/jordan)

---

> "It's making design more accessible to people. That's a really exciting opportunity."

— Jordan Singer, on AI democratizing design, quoted in Mercury profile (mercury.com/blog/jordan-singer)

---

> "Design will be so accessible that anyone will be able to visualize their ideas with low effort."

— Jordan Singer, on his long-term vision, as reported in the Not Boring Founder's Letter (notboring.co)

---

> "The core ways we interact with computers haven't kept pace with technological advancements. While software has grown more powerful, it's still reactive — requiring manual input at every step."

— Jordan Singer, Mainframe launch interview, Pulse2 / AlleyWatch, 2024

---

> "At Mainframe, we're imagining a future where AI is the default, software generates itself and agents are the new apps."

— Jordan Singer, Mainframe communications, 2024

---

> "The missing ingredients for getting the most out of agents are making them accessible, collaborative, and familiar."

— Jordan Singer, "Say Hello to Cobot," blog.mainfra.me, 2024

---

> "Make small teams just as capable and powerful as larger ones by dramatically increasing what your team can accomplish."

— Jordan Singer, describing Mainframe's mission, "Say Hello to Cobot," blog.mainfra.me, 2024

---

Note: Several commonly cited paraphrases of Singer's views (on AI replacing designers, on design accessibility) appear in secondary sources summarizing interviews rather than as direct quotations. Only statements clearly attributed as direct quotes have been included above. Readers researching Singer's views in depth should consult his Substack archive (ibuildmyideas.substack.com), his Twitter/X profile (@jsngr), and the Mercury/Meridian profile interview directly.
