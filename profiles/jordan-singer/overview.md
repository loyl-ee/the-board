# Jordan Singer

## Who He Is

Jordan Singer is a designer-developer hybrid who has spent roughly a decade at the intersection of consumer software craft, iOS/macOS development, and — since 2020 — AI-native design tools. He describes himself simply as "I build my ideas," a phrase he registered as a domain in 2015 while still in college and which has become his consistent personal brand.

His most significant professional chapter was founding Diagram, one of the earliest startups to natively embed AI into the design workflow. Diagram's tools — Magician (AI-powered icon and copy generation inside Figma), Genius (an AI design companion that autocompleted designs using a project's own component library), and Automator (a no-code plugin builder for Figma) — accumulated roughly 100,000 users before Figma acquired the company in June 2023. The Diagram team of five joined Figma, where Singer served as a product designer and AI lead, helping shape features including the Make Designs capability announced at Config 2024. He departed Figma in September 2024, approximately fourteen months after the acquisition.

After leaving Figma, Singer co-founded Mainframe with Lou Mata and Shivkanth Bagavathy. Mainframe is building generative productivity software; its first product, Cobot, is a proactive AI assistant designed to anticipate user needs rather than wait for commands. The company raised a $5.5M seed round co-led by Lachy Groom and Peter Boyce (Stellation) in late 2024, bringing total funding to $6.75M.

## Background

Singer is largely self-taught in both design and engineering. He discovered web development in the 2000s by reverse-engineering sites he admired — he cites Apple's early 2000s website specifically as a formative influence, noting its intentional typography, layout, and almost artisanal craftsmanship. He attended the University of Arizona and studied computer science. Before founding Diagram, he spent approximately four years at Square working on the Cash App team as a product designer. He joined Square specifically to understand how design operates at scale, and found that his engineering background made him unusually effective at building real, working prototypes.

## Position in the Field

Singer occupies a specific and relatively rare position: a designer who writes production-quality Swift/SwiftUI and JavaScript, who launched multiple consumer iOS apps (the lil software line), who built Figma plugins from first principles, and who then turned that accumulated knowledge into a company at the frontier of AI design tooling. His trajectory — prolific side-project builder to funded AI startup founder to Figma employee to serial founder again — makes him a useful reference point for any decision involving AI-native product design, small focused tools, or the question of what design craft looks like when AI is a core part of the workflow.

His GitHub profile (github.com/jordansinger) has 2,700+ followers and includes popular open-source repositories: SwiftUI-Kit (2,500+ stars), Hook (1,600+ stars), and UIDraw, a mobile app for drawing and generating websites. He is active on X/Twitter as @jsngr.

## Honest Gaps

Singer has not published long-form essays in the manner of, say, Oliver Reichenstein or John Gruber. Most of his documented thinking comes from interviews, newsletter posts on his ibuildmyideas.substack.com, tweets, and podcast appearances. His views on topics like AI safety, responsible design, and UX methodology are largely inferred from his product decisions and interview comments rather than explicit position statements.
