# Jordan Singer — On Business

## Building in Public as a Distribution Strategy

Singer's business model for Diagram started with audience-building, not fundraising. During the COVID-19 lockdown in 2020, he began shipping experimental iOS apps publicly on Twitter — the lil software line — and documenting the process as he went. By the time he announced Diagram and a $3M seed round in May 2022, he had accumulated more than 50,000 Twitter followers drawn to his combination of design craft and technical output. That pre-existing audience became Diagram's first user base, giving the company an unusual head start on distribution for a design tools startup.

This is a deliberate strategy. Singer has observed that designer-founded companies are rare because investors often undervalue design as a differentiator and because designers tend toward risk aversion. His approach to this problem was to reduce reliance on investor attention by building an audience directly.

## Diagram: The Small Tool as Viable Product

Diagram demonstrated that a team of five people building focused AI design plugins could reach 100,000 users and command the attention of Figma — a company valued at $20 billion at the time of acquisition. The company raised a $3M seed round with participation from Figma Ventures itself, which created a natural acqui-hire pathway.

Singer shared Diagram's original 2022 pitch deck publicly on Twitter a year after the acquisition, a characteristic act of transparency. The deck framed Diagram as a platform for powering UI design through generative AI, with a vision that AI would eventually automate the tedious and repetitive parts of design workflows so designers could focus on higher-order decisions.

The acquisition terms were not publicly disclosed.

## The Diagram-to-Figma Transition

Singer joined Figma as a product designer and AI lead following the acquisition. He and Marco Cornacchia (Diagram co-founder) both departed Figma in September 2024, approximately fourteen months after the acquisition closed. Their departure coincided with controversy around the Make Designs feature — an AI tool that generated designs resembling existing Apple apps, which Figma temporarily pulled and publicly acknowledged had insufficient quality assurance. Singer's precise reasons for leaving have not been publicly detailed.

## Mainframe: The Next Chapter

After leaving Figma, Singer co-founded Mainframe with a clear thesis: current software is reactive, requiring manual input at every step, and the coming generation of software should anticipate user needs through AI agents. The company's first product, Cobot, is positioned as an agent-native, multiplayer productivity tool. Its $5.5M seed round (late 2024) targets the $60B+ global productivity software market.

Singer's stated competitive differentiation for Mainframe is the combination of sophisticated AI with design quality: "Mainframe bridges the gap between advanced AI and intuitive design. Unlike other tools that simply automate tasks, Cobot works as a collaborative partner."

## Views on the Design Tools Market

Singer has argued that the role of a designer will be "accelerated by design automation so they can focus on what matters and less on tedious stuff." He sees design tools as undergoing a transition analogous to the early 1900s manufacturing assembly line — where processes that once took half a day were compressed to 90 minutes. He believes the design tools market is still early in this transition.
