# Jordan Singer — On Web

## Web Presence and Distribution Channels

Singer distributes his work through a focused set of web channels, each serving a distinct audience and purpose.

**ibuildmyideas.com** is his primary personal site and personal brand anchor. The domain was registered in 2015 and has been the consistent home of his identity across all his projects. The site is minimal — characteristically consistent with his design philosophy — and functions primarily as a landing page pointing to his active projects and social profiles.

**X/Twitter (@jsngr)** is his primary real-time distribution channel. Twitter is where his audience was built during the lil software era of 2020-2021, where his viral GPT-3 Figma plugin demonstration ("This changes everything. With GPT-3, I built a Figma plugin to design for you") reached far outside his existing following, and where he has continued to announce new work, share in-progress experiments, and engage with the designer-developer community.

**Figma Community (figma.com/@jordan)** served as distribution for his Figma plugins during the Tricycle AI and Diagram periods. The Figma plugin marketplace provided a captive audience of active Figma users who could install plugins without leaving the tool they were already using — a significant distribution advantage for design tool products.

**GitHub (github.com/jordansinger)** functions as both a technical portfolio and a community-building mechanism. Popular repositories attract ongoing stars and forks from developers who discover the work independently of Singer's social media presence.

**Substack** hosts both his personal newsletter (ibuildmyideas.substack.com) and the Mainframe blog (blog.mainfra.me), providing a longer-form channel for documenting product philosophy and company updates.

## Approach to Web-Based Tool Distribution

Singer's experience with App Store rejection for several lil software apps (lil notes, lil todo, lil browser, lil bookmarks) informed his thinking about distribution risk. He built Airport partly as a response: a TestFlight discovery platform that created a web-based community for apps that couldn't or hadn't yet reached the App Store. This demonstrated an understanding that distribution constraints shape product strategy — a lesson he carried into Diagram, where distributing as Figma plugins rather than standalone apps reduced friction significantly.

Mainframe is web-distributed as a SaaS product with tiered subscription plans, representing a shift toward the recurring-revenue model typical of B2B productivity tools rather than the one-time or free App Store model of his earlier consumer work.

## Honest Gaps

Singer has not published detailed writing about web strategy, SEO, or digital marketing. His audience-building appears to have been driven primarily by the quality and novelty of his public work rather than deliberate web distribution strategy.
