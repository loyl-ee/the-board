# Jordan Singer — On Customer Journey

## Building an Audience Before Building a Company

Singer's relationship with his users is unusual in that it predates the existence of any company. He began building an audience on Twitter during the COVID-19 lockdown of 2020 by sharing his lil software apps and experimental Figma plugins publicly as he built them. This build-in-public approach attracted designers and developers who were interested in the overlap of Swift, Figma, and emerging AI capabilities. By the time he announced Diagram's $3M seed round in 2022, he had over 50,000 Twitter followers who already understood his work and trusted his taste.

This sequence — audience first, company second — gave Diagram an unusual distribution advantage. Its first users were not cold-acquired; they were warm converts from an existing community who had watched Singer build and had strong prior credibility with his work.

## Product Hunt and Designer Community Engagement

Singer has launched products on Product Hunt and within the Figma Community (figma.com/@jordan), using both platforms as distribution channels for his plugins and tools. His Figma Community profile accumulated followers through both the quality of his shared plugin work and his reputation on Twitter.

The Magician plugin specifically benefited from community-driven growth: designers discovering it through Figma's plugin marketplace, Twitter sharing, and design community newsletters. Diagram's 100,000 users were largely acquired through these organic channels rather than paid growth.

## Open Source as Community Building

Singer's GitHub presence (68 repositories, 2,700+ followers) functions as a parallel community-building mechanism. His SwiftUI-Kit repository (2,500+ stars) and Hook JavaScript library (1,600+ stars) are widely referenced by iOS and web developers respectively. Open-sourcing polished, production-quality code creates a different kind of trust than a product launch — it demonstrates competence in a language that technical audiences respect.

This technical credibility made his AI design tools more trustworthy to designer-developers than comparable tools from teams without similar open-source track records.

## Newsletter: ibuildmyideas.substack.com

Singer maintained a public newsletter (I Build My Ideas) where he documented his projects and thinking in progress. Posts covered specific projects (Automator for Figma, the lil software line, Airport), his process and philosophy, and his evolving views on AI in design. This newsletter served as the primary long-form record of his thinking prior to Diagram and remains a useful archive of his early-stage product reasoning. After Diagram, he launched the Mainframe blog on Substack (blog.mainfra.me) focused on "generative computing."

## Post-Acquisition Transparency

Singer shared Diagram's original 2022 pitch deck publicly on Twitter a year after the Figma acquisition, with a Loom recording walkthrough. This act of transparency — exposing the strategic reasoning and framing of a pre-acquisition startup — is characteristic of his open-building ethos and generated significant engagement from the design and startup communities.
