# Ethan Mollick — Verified Quotes

The quotes below are drawn from Mollick's published essays, his book *Co-Intelligence*, and documented interviews. Where a direct source URL or publication is available, it is cited. Quotes drawn from book summaries on Goodreads or secondary summaries have been flagged; only those with high source confidence are included.

---

## From *Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI* (2024)

> "You should try inviting AI to help you in everything you do, barring legal or ethical barriers. As you experiment, you may find that AI help can be satisfying, or frustrating, or useless, or unnerving."
— *Co-Intelligence*, Chapter on Principle 1 (2024)

> "Imagine your AI collaborator as an infinitely fast intern, eager to please but prone to bending the truth."
— *Co-Intelligence* (2024), via Goodreads

> "LLMs are not generally optimized to say 'I don't know' when they don't have enough information."
— *Co-Intelligence* (2024), via Goodreads

> "You can no longer trust that anything you see, or hear, or read was not created by AI."
— *Co-Intelligence* (2024), via Goodreads

> "In field after field, we are finding that humans working with an AI co-intelligence outperforms all but the best humans working without AI."
— *Co-Intelligence* (2024), via Goodreads

> "Whatever you are best at, you are very likely better than the best LLM at that same skill. This is why, at least for now, AI use can feel liberating."
— *Co-Intelligence* (2024), via Goodreads

— "Thinking Like an AI," *One Useful Thing*, October 20, 2024

> "Several billion people just got free interns. They are weird, somewhat alien interns that work infinitely fast."
— "On-Boarding Your AI Intern," *One Useful Thing*, May 20, 2023

> "The AI you are using is the worst and least capable AI you will ever use."
— "On-Boarding Your AI Intern," *One Useful Thing*, May 20, 2023

## From Interviews and Public Appearances

> "Working with AI is easiest if you think of it like an alien person rather than a human-built machine."
— "On the Necessity of a Sin," cited by Simon Willison, March 2024

> "Everything is going to happen all at once. We need to be modeling good examples."
— TIME Magazine interview, "The 100 Most Influential People in AI 2024," September 2024 (also shared by Mollick on X, @emollick, September 5, 2024)

> "Every company is full of secret cyborgs. When I give talks, people always come up afterwards to confess they have automated their work and don't want to tell anyone."
— @emollick, X/Twitter, August 7, 2024

> "Big issue in organizations: They have put together elaborate rules for AI use focused on negative use cases. As a result, employees are too scared to talk about how they use AI, or to use corporate LLMs. They just become secret cyborgs, using their own AI and not sharing knowledge."
— @emollick, X/Twitter, August 28, 2024

> "If the output is what matters in your business, you're in trouble of being bitter lessoned. If the process matters — the conversations, the writing of the report more than the report itself — then there's hope."
— Interview with Insight Partners, 2024

> "You don't know what AI is good for or bad for inside your job or your industry. Nobody knows."
— Stanford Graduate School of Business, "Co-Intelligence: An AI Masterclass," 2024

> "Instructors cannot detect AI writing reliably: detectors don't work and accuse non-native speakers of cheating."
— @emollick, X/Twitter, September 19, 2023
