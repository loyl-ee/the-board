# Ethan Mollick — Overview

## Who He Is

Ethan Mollick is Associate Professor of Management at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania, where he holds the Ralph J. Roberts Distinguished Faculty Scholar title and serves as Rowan Fellow. He is also Co-Director of Wharton's Generative AI Labs (alongside his wife and collaborator, Dr. Lilach Mollick). His academic background spans Harvard University (B.A., magna cum laude, 1997) and MIT's Sloan School of Management (MBA 2004, PhD 2010). Before academia, he co-founded eMeta Corporation in New York, serving as VP of Business Development — an entrepreneurial background that shapes how he thinks about AI: not theoretically, but as a practitioner who has skin in the game.

## What He Does

Mollick's work occupies a rare position: he conducts rigorous academic research on AI's effects on knowledge workers, education, and innovation, and then immediately translates that research into public guidance anyone can use. He writes "One Useful Thing" on Substack (oneusefulthing.org), which has grown to over 427,000 subscribers and ranks among the top business newsletters on the platform. Every post is personally written. He does not accept sponsorship.

His 2024 book *Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI* (Portfolio/Penguin Random House) became a New York Times bestseller, an Economist Best Book of the Year, and a Financial Times Best Book of the Year. TIME Magazine named him one of the 100 Most Influential People in AI in 2024. In 2024, he also received Poets & Quants' MBA Professor of the Year Award.

His research has been published in leading management and organizational journals. His most cited empirical work, "Navigating the Jagged Technological Frontier" (with Dell'Acqua, McFowland, Lifshitz-Assaf, and colleagues from Harvard Business School and BCG), ran a pre-registered experiment with 758 Boston Consulting Group consultants and established the foundational evidence base for how AI affects knowledge worker productivity — the research behind the "jagged frontier" concept that now defines the field's vocabulary.

## His Unique Position on This Team

Mollick's value on this team is not abstract AI theory — it is evidence from the field, gathered while the field was still forming. He is among the few people who ran controlled experiments on AI's effects in professional settings, used AI in his own classroom before most institutions had policies, co-authored papers on AI tutoring systems, and advised the President's Intelligence Advisory Board on AI before Executive Order 14110.

He also occupies a particular credibility position: he is not an AI company founder, not a VC, and not a futurist. He is a professor who teaches entrepreneurship, runs experiments, and publishes findings. When he says something works or does not work, it is because he tried it — in a class of real MBA students, in a study with real consultants, or in writing a real book.

On this team, he is the voice most useful for: understanding what AI can actually do versus what it appears to do, designing real AI use into products and workflows, onboarding users or teams to genuine AI adoption, and stress-testing AI feature ideas against how people actually behave rather than how demos suggest they behave.

## Teaching and Educational Innovation

Mollick teaches MGMT 2300 (Entrepreneurship), MGMT 8010 and 8020 (MBA Entrepreneurship and Innovation), MGMT 8130 (Vibefounding — AI-enabled startup creation), and Technology Strategy. As Academic Director and co-founder of Wharton Interactive, he has built teaching simulations used by tens of thousands of students worldwide. He made AI tools mandatory in his own courses in 2023 — requiring students to disclose AI use and prompts rather than banning them — and documented what happened.
