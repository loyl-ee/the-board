# Sal Soghoian — On Web

## macosxautomation.com as Educational Infrastructure

Soghoian has maintained macosxautomation.com since his Apple years and continued to operate it after his departure. His stated purpose is direct: to ensure that quality, usable information about user automation is freely available to anyone who needs it, with particular attention to accessibility for users with disabilities.

The site is organized as a reference and tutorial resource covering AppleScript, Automator, Services, JavaScript for Automation, shell scripting, and later Shortcuts and Omni Automation. Unlike commercial documentation, it prioritizes practical working examples over comprehensive API coverage — the site's value proposition is that a user can find a working script or workflow for their specific task, not just a language reference. Soghoian has consistently provided working code that can be copied, modified, and put directly to use.

The site also functions as a record of his thinking over time. Articles written during his Apple tenure coexist with post-Apple material, giving researchers and practitioners a longitudinal view of how automation on the Mac has evolved and how Soghoian's own perspective on it has developed.

## YouTube and Video Tutorials

Soghoian's "Mac OS X Automation" YouTube presence extends his educational mission into video format, covering specific automation techniques with working demonstrations. His approach is hands-on and practical: showing a real problem, building a real solution, and explaining the design decisions along the way. The format aligns with his broader educational philosophy — users learn automation by seeing it work, not by reading about it abstractly.

## Post-Apple Web Writing

After leaving Apple in late 2016, Soghoian's first significant public writing appeared at MacStories, where his January 2017 essay "App Extensions Are Not a Replacement for User Automation" set out his position on the direction Apple's automation strategy was taking. He later contributed to Rebooting (Substack) with a longer-form interview covering his history at Apple, the Automator origin story, and his views on Shortcuts.

## Gap Note

Soghoian's web presence is primarily educational and documentary rather than conversational. He does not maintain an active blog in the conventional sense, and his social media presence is limited. His most substantial and current content tends to appear in podcast interviews rather than in written web form. The automationorchard.com domain appears in search results associated with AppleScript resources but does not appear to be a project he is directly running.
