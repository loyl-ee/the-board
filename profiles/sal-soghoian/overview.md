# Sal Soghoian — Overview

## Who He Is

Sal Soghoian is the foremost advocate in Apple's history for placing the power of computing directly in the hands of the people using computers. From January 1997 through October 2016, he served as Apple's Product Manager for Automation Technologies — a nearly twenty-year tenure that made him the longest-serving person in that role. During that span he was the institutional force behind AppleScript, Automator, System Services, JavaScript for Automation (JXA), the UNIX command-line environment, and Apple Configurator. No single individual has done more to ensure that Mac users could bend their computers to their own will.

Before Apple, Soghoian built automation systems for the publishing industry in the 1990s, discovering the transformative power of scripting while solving real production problems for media companies. He created "Sal's AppleScript Snippets," the ShadowCaster Quark XTension, and co-authored the book *AppleScript 1-2-3*. He was an AppleScript evangelist before he was an Apple employee.

## The Automator Story

In late 2002, Soghoian joined an informal skunkworks team developing a workflow automation application. After eighteen months of development — built mostly as a side effort — he sat outside Steve Jobs's demo room in building three at Apple, waiting for an opening to show it. When Jobs finally saw the application, his response was immediate: "You want to be in the show?" One week later, Soghoian was on stage at WWDC 2004 introducing Automator to the world. It shipped as part of Mac OS X Tiger in 2005 and remains in macOS today. Soghoian holds co-inventor status on U.S. Patent #7428535 for the technology. Jobs, notably, consistently called him "Saul" — even from the WWDC stage.

## After Apple

In November 2016, Apple eliminated the Product Manager of Automation Technologies position for business reasons. Soghoian announced this at the MacTech Conference in Los Angeles, closing with the words: "I joined Apple in January of 1997, almost twenty years ago, because of my profound belief that 'the power of the computer should reside in the hands of the one using it.' That credo remains my truth to this day."

After leaving, he began contributing to MacStories, launched the CMD-D: Masters of Automation Conference (2017), and partnered with The Omni Group to architect Omni Automation — a JavaScript-based, cross-platform automation framework running inside their suite of apps. He continues to maintain macosxautomation.com as a free educational resource for the automation community, and has appeared repeatedly on the Mac Power Users podcast (episodes 370, 588, 815) and the Automators podcast to advocate for user scripting capabilities.

## Why He Is on This Team

Soghoian represents a philosophy that is both technically specific and morally grounded: automation is a form of user freedom, not a developer convenience. He spent two decades building the institutional infrastructure for power users to orchestrate their computers, and he paid for that belief when his position was eliminated. His presence on this team guards against any design decision that treats automation as an afterthought, that locks users into a predetermined experience, or that confuses consumer simplicity with genuine user power. When the question is whether an app should be scriptable, whether Shortcuts support should be real or superficial, or whether power users are worth designing for — Soghoian is the voice to consult first.
