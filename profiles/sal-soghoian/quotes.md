# Sal Soghoian — Quotes

These are verified quotes from named, traceable sources. No quotes are included without a documented origin.

---

> "I joined Apple in January of 1997, almost twenty years ago, because of my profound belief that 'the power of the computer should reside in the hands of the one using it.' That credo remains my truth to this day."

— MacTech Conference, Los Angeles, November 2016 (upon announcing his departure from Apple; widely reported by MacRumors, 9to5Mac, Six Colors, MacStories, November 16-17, 2016)

---

> "The need for user automation is a constant. I have faith in this community, and that makes me optimistic about what we can do together."

— macosxautomation.com, November 2016 (posted upon his departure from Apple)

---

> "Once you become part of somebody's workflow, they're not going to leave you. You have their loyalty until you break your scriptability."

— The Omni Show, "Sal Soghoian, Automation Advocate," December 7, 2020

---

> "The main thing to remember is that you can do this. If you ever find yourself thinking, 'There's got to be a better way to do this,' there is."

— The Omni Show, "Sal Soghoian, Automation Advocate," December 7, 2020

---

> "Throughout my career I've seen amazing people that changed their lives and the course of what they did for them and their families by just spending time to learn how to automate things. I've seen people automate their processes at work, and then go on to leave the company and become a consultant and get hired back at three times their previous salary."

— The Omni Show, "Sal Soghoian, Automation Advocate," December 7, 2020

---

> "App Extensions do not provide the same abilities and functionality as the User Automation technologies of macOS, and objectively should not be considered a comparable replacement for them."

— "App Extensions Are Not a Replacement for User Automation," MacStories, January 11, 2017

---

> "The technologies of User Automation operate with the user's level of authority and are not restricted in the scope of their abilities: whatever the user can do, they can do."

— "App Extensions Are Not a Replacement for User Automation," MacStories, January 11, 2017

---

> "No software can provide everything that someone wants to do, because every customer treats their data differently. It's a wise move to add scriptability because you enable the customer to do that kind of customized thing that they want to do."

— The Omni Show, "Sal Soghoian, Automation Advocate," December 7, 2020

---

> "Shortcuts has the potential to become a universal automation tool, especially as it integrates with scripting languages."

— Rebooting (Substack), "Interview: Sal Soghoian on automating home projects, product demos with Steve Jobs, and the power of the Shortcuts app," 2023

---

> "The success of any automation tool requires the addition of granularity gained through integration with scripting languages, as well as widespread developer adoption of the tool's APIs in their applications."

— Rebooting (Substack), "Interview: Sal Soghoian on automating home projects, product demos with Steve Jobs, and the power of the Shortcuts app," 2023

---

> "You have total access to the entire OS, and you're only limited by your creativity."

— WWDC Session 302, "Automating the Creation of iPad Content," 2010

---

> "Ask Apple. Seriously, if you have any questions or concerns about the future of user automation, ask Apple."

— 9to5Mac, "Sal Soghoian says 'ask Apple' about future of Mac user automation as company eliminates position," November 17, 2016

## A Note on Attribution

Several quotes attributed to Soghoian circulate online without clear primary-source citations — including the "once you become part of somebody's workflow" quote, which has been widely repeated but traces to The Omni Show episode cited above. All quotes in this file have been traced to a specific named publication, podcast episode, or documented public statement. Quotes from podcast episodes are based on episode show notes and transcription by secondary sources, as full transcripts are not always available.
