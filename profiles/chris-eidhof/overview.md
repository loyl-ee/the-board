# Chris Eidhof — Overview

## Who He Is

Chris Eidhof is a Dutch software developer based in Germany (Berlin, and later Fürstenberg/Havel), best known as co-founder of [objc.io](https://www.objc.io/), one of the most respected technical publishing operations in the Apple developer community. He is a practitioner-educator who has spent over a decade translating difficult ideas in Swift and functional programming into approachable, rigorous content through books, weekly videos, interactive guides, and workshops.

## Origins

Eidhof grew up in the Netherlands and studied at Utrecht University, where he immersed himself in Haskell and functional programming alongside a close-knit group of students. He also worked extensively with JavaScript and built early websites in PHP and HTML/CSS. When Apple released the first public iPhone SDK, he pivoted to iOS development and never left.

In 2012, frustrated by the absence of English-language iOS conferences in Germany, he moved to Berlin and co-organized UIKonf alongside Matt and Peter — one of Europe's earliest independent iOS developer conferences. "Seeing the first edition was pure magic," he has noted about that experience.

## objc.io

In 2013, Eidhof co-founded objc.io with Florian Kugler and Daniel Eggert. The stated mission: "to create a platform for in-depth technical topics relevant to all iOS and macOS developers." For two years they published 24 monthly issues featuring 125 articles by 67 authors, building a mailing list approaching 7,000 subscribers within five months. In 2015, they pivoted to long-form books, and in 2016 launched Swift Talk — a weekly video series that has produced over 488 episodes across more than nine years of uninterrupted weekly publishing.

Today, objc.io operates as a small independent publishing and workshop business run primarily by Eidhof and Kugler. Its output includes multiple self-published books, the Swift Talk subscription video series, in-person and remote workshops for engineering teams, and interactive web guides.

## Books

Eidhof has co-authored or co-edited five major technical books:

- **Functional Swift** (with Florian Kugler and Wouter Swierstra, 2014) — applies functional programming concepts pragmatically to Swift; updated through Swift 5.
- **Advanced Swift** (with Ole Begemann, Florian Kugler, and Ben Cohen, 2015) — covers the full range of the language from low-level C interop to generics; updated through Swift 5.6.
- **Core Data** (with Florian Kugler and Daniel Eggert, 2015) — deep dive into Apple's persistence framework.
- **App Architecture** (with Matt Gallagher and Florian Kugler, 2018) — surveys MVC, MVVM, and experimental patterns including The Elm Architecture.
- **Thinking in SwiftUI** (with Florian Kugler, 2020, updated for iOS 17) — conceptual guide to SwiftUI's layout and state systems.

## Swift Talk and the SwiftUI Field Guide

Swift Talk launched in June 2016 as a weekly live-coding video series. Episodes run 17–29 minutes; half are free and half require a $9/month subscription. As of 2025, the series has produced more than 488 episodes covering SwiftUI (219+ episodes), parsers, architecture, networking, server-side Swift, and more.

In 2024, Eidhof built the [SwiftUI Field Guide](https://www.swiftuifieldguide.com/) — a free interactive web guide that reimplements key parts of SwiftUI's layout system in TypeScript to create visual, manipulable explanations. It reflects his conviction that documentation should be visual and interactive, not just textual.

## Presence

Eidhof is active on Mastodon at @chris@m.objc.io. He was previously on Twitter as @chriseidhof before stepping back. He speaks at conferences including UIKonf and NSSpain and has given talks on topics ranging from functional view controllers to building programming languages in Swift.
