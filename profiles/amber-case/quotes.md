# Amber Case — Quotes

A curated set of verifiable quotes from Amber Case, drawn from her book, talks, podcast appearances, and published interviews. Fabricated quotes have been excluded; where a quote is paraphrased in secondary sources rather than directly attributed, it has been noted.

---

> "Calm technology is the idea that the scarce resource in the 21st century is not technology, it's our attention."

— FUTURES Podcast, Episode 9 (published interview)

---

> "A person's primary task should not be computing, but being human."

— *Calm Technology: Principles and Patterns for Non-Intrusive Design*, O'Reilly, 2015 (Principle II); also stated at calmtech.com

---

> "The right amount of technology is the minimum needed to solve the problem."

— *Calm Technology: Principles and Patterns for Non-Intrusive Design*, O'Reilly, 2015 (Principle VII); also stated at calmtech.com

---

> "Calm technology describes a state of technological maturity where a user's primary task is not computing, but being human."

— *Calm Technology: Principles and Patterns for Non-Intrusive Design*, O'Reilly, 2015; repeated in NEXT Conference talk, 2018

---

> "Machines shouldn't act like humans and humans shouldn't act like machines."

— Mind the Product conference talk (published on mindtheproduct.com)

---

> "The stress you experience from the technology is by design — either because designers did not fully prioritize your attention and humanity."

— "Designing Tech That (Finally) Respects Our Time & Humanity," Medium / Calm Tech Institute announcement, 2024

---

> "Our goal with the Calm Tech Institute is to completely change what we expect from technology."

— "Designing Tech That (Finally) Respects Our Time & Humanity," Medium / Calm Tech Institute announcement, 2024

---

> "Technology can communicate, but it doesn't need to speak."

— *Calm Technology: Principles and Patterns for Non-Intrusive Design*, O'Reilly, 2015 (Principle V); also stated in UX Podcast Episode 122 transcript

---

> "The idea of intelligent technology is a misnomer. Technology should inform us so that we can make our best decisions."

— UX Podcast, Episode 122 ("Calm Technology with Amber Case"), transcript published on Medium

---

> "The fundamental issue behind all of this is that we have companies now that are larger than entire countries. It's a threat to them to say, 'Hey, use our services less, and we're going to help you.'"

— Future Thinkers Podcast, interview on calm technology and human-machine interaction

---

> "What we're looking at is not an extension of the physical self, but an extension of the mental self."

— TEDWomen 2010, "We Are All Cyborgs Now" (widely cited paraphrase of the talk's core argument; appears in multiple summaries and the TED description)

---

> "A hammer is an extension of our fist; a knife an extension of our teeth; and cave paintings, an extension of our brain."

— FUTURES Podcast, Episode 9; also cited in multiple secondary sources summarizing her cyborg anthropology framework

---

> "The thing we would have the least of would be our own attention, and how we can make technology that respected that attention instead of took it away would be a really important thing to work on in the future."

— Future Thinkers Podcast, interview on calm technology

---

> "Our world is made of information that competes for our attention. What is necessary? What is not?"

— calmtech.com (site introduction text, attributed to Amber Case as author of the site)

---

**Source note**: Verbatim quotes from the full *Calm Technology* book text are not publicly reproduced in quotable form in available web sources. The quotes attributed to the book above appear on the book's official site (calmtech.com) and in the O'Reilly description, and are consistent with Case's stated principles across multiple sources. Readers seeking additional verbatim book quotes should consult the O'Reilly edition directly.
