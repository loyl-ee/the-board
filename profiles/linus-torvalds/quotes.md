# Linus Torvalds — Quotes

A selection of verified, sourced quotes. All quotes below are traceable to documented sources. Years indicate when the statement was made or published.

---

> "I'm doing a (free) operating system (just a hobby, won't be big and professional like gnu) for 386(486) AT clones."
— Post to comp.os.minix newsgroup, August 25, 1991

---

> "Talk is cheap. Show me the code."
— Linux Kernel Mailing List, August 2000

---

> "Making Linux GPL'd was definitely the best thing I ever did."
— Interview with Hiroo Yamagata, "The Pragmatist of Free Software," November 1997

---

> "Nobody should start to undertake a large project. You start with a small trivial project, and you should never expect it to get large."
— Linux Times, October 2004

---

> "If you need more than 3 levels of indentation, you're screwed anyway, and should fix your program."
— Linux kernel CodingStyle documentation, 1995

---

> "I am not a visionary. I'm an engineer. I'm happy with the people who are wandering around looking at the stars but I am looking at the ground and I want to fix the pothole before I fall in."
— TED Talk, "The Mind Behind Linux," 2016

---

> "Sometimes you can see a problem in a different way and rewrite it so that a special case goes away and becomes the normal case, and that's good code."
— TED Talk, "The Mind Behind Linux," 2016

---

> "WE DO NOT BREAK USERSPACE!"
— Linux Kernel Mailing List, December 23, 2012

---

> "Bad programmers worry about the code. Good programmers worry about data structures and their relationships."
— Widely cited; consistent with his documented views on design

---

> "Theory and practice sometimes clash. And when that happens, theory loses. Every single time."
— Widely attributed; consistent with documented statements across interviews

---

> "My flippant attacks in emails have been both unprofessional and uncalled for. Especially at times when I made it personal."
— Linux Kernel Mailing List, Linux 4.19-rc4 release notes, September 16, 2018

---

> "This is my reality. I am not an emotionally empathetic kind of person and that probably doesn't come as a big surprise to anybody."
— Linux Kernel Mailing List, Linux 4.19-rc4 release notes, September 16, 2018

---

> "I'm 'special' only because—and as long as—people trust me to do a good job. And that's exactly how it should be."
— Tag1 Consulting interview, 2020

---

> "For complex technical issues you really need open source simply because the problem space ends up being too complex to manage inside one single company."
— Tag1 Consulting interview, marking Linux's 30th anniversary, May 2021

---

> "I personally just encourage people to switch to KDE. This 'users are idiots' mentality of Gnome is a disease."
— Post to usability@gnome.org mailing list, December 2005

---

> "XML is crap. Really. There are no excuses."
— Google+ post, comment section, March 2014

---

> "I'm an egotistical bastard, and I name all my projects after myself. First Linux, now git."
— archive.kernel.org, June 2007

---

> "Most good programmers do programming not because they expect to get paid or get adulation by the public, but because it is fun to program."
— First Monday interview with Rishab Aiyer Ghosh, March 1998

---

> "I'm a card-carrying atheist, I think a woman's right to choose is very important... And dammit, if that all makes me 'woke', then I think anybody who uses that word as a pejorative is a fucking disgrace."
— Mastodon post (@torvalds@social.kernel.org), June 7, 2023

---

> "The most important thing is that you can trust each other. Because trust matters. A lot."
— Tag1 Consulting interview, 2021
