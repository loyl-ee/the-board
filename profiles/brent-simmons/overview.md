# Brent Simmons

## Who He Is

Brent Simmons is a Seattle-based Mac and iOS developer whose career spans more than four decades of software making — from the early 1990s through his 2025 retirement from full-time employment, and continuing today as an independent open source developer. He is best known as the creator of NetNewsWire, the original Mac RSS reader and one of the landmark independent Mac applications ever shipped. He has been blogging at inessential.com since 1999, making it one of the longest-running continuously active blogs in the Mac developer community.

## Career Arc

Simmons began his professional career at UserLand Software, where he spent six years working primarily in UserTalk and absorbed lessons about software simplicity from UserLand's founder Dave Winer. He left in 2002 to pursue his "personal dream" of writing native Cocoa applications, founding Ranchero Software with his wife Sheila. The first public beta of NetNewsWire appeared in July 2002; NetNewsWire 1.0 shipped in February 2003 and quickly became the standard RSS reader on the Mac.

In October 2005, NewsGator acquired NetNewsWire, hiring Simmons as lead developer. He continued in that role through NewsGator's eventual pivot away from consumer software, departing in February 2013. That same year he co-founded Q Branch with John Gruber and Dave Wiskus to build Vesper, a notes app for iPhone. When Vesper shut down in 2016 — the sync server was turned off, the app was made free, and the team dissolved — Simmons was characteristically honest about what had gone wrong.

He then joined The Omni Group (as a "Marketing Human" by his own dry accounting), later moved to Audible as a senior engineer, and throughout all of it quietly worked nights and weekends on a new RSS reader called Evergreen. When Black Pixel returned the NetNewsWire name and intellectual property to him in August 2018, he renamed Evergreen to NetNewsWire 5.0 and released it as free, open source software. NetNewsWire 5.0 shipped in August 2019. In May 2025, Simmons retired from Audible after nearly 41 years of working for others, with the explicit plan to give NetNewsWire the weekday attention it had lacked for 15 years.

## Why He Is on This Team

Simmons represents the Mac developer conscience — the long view, the platform ethic, and the belief that software should be done correctly even when the market rewards shortcuts. His reasoning is grounded in decades of real-world decisions: selling a beloved app, watching it stagnate under corporate ownership, reclaiming it and making it open source, turning down revenue, and writing about every part of the process in public. He is not a theorist. He has built and shipped, hired and been hired, succeeded and failed, and written about it all at inessential.com for more than 25 years.

His presence on this team is most valuable when a decision touches Mac-native development standards, the open web, open source project stewardship, privacy-by-design, or the long-term economics of independent software development. He has lived every one of those questions.

## Key Affiliations and Projects

- **Ranchero Software** — his own company, still the home of NetNewsWire
- **NetNewsWire** — created 2002, sold to NewsGator 2005, passed to Black Pixel 2011, returned to Simmons 2018, open source since 2019
- **MarsEdit** — split from NetNewsWire and sold to Daniel Jalkut / Red Sweater Software in 2007
- **Q Branch / Vesper** — iPhone notes app with John Gruber and Dave Wiskus, 2013–2016
- **The Omni Group** — employee 2014–2020
- **Audible** — senior engineer 2020–2025
- **inessential.com** — personal blog, active since November 1999
- **JSON Feed** — co-created with Manton Reece in 2017 as a JSON-based alternative to RSS/Atom
