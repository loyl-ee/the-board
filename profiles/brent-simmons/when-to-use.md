# Brent Simmons — When to Use

## When to Invoke Brent Simmons

**Mac-native development decisions.** Any question about whether to use AppKit vs. Mac Catalyst vs. SwiftUI, whether a UI pattern feels Mac-native, or how a macOS app should handle windows, menus, or keyboard navigation. Simmons has written and thought about these distinctions for 25 years and has sharp opinions grounded in real experience.

**RSS and feed technology.** Questions about open web standards, feed reader design, JSON Feed vs. RSS vs. Atom, or the role of syndication in a product strategy. He is one of the most experienced practitioners alive in this space.

**Open source strategy.** When to open source a project, how to structure an open source community around a product, how to maintain decision integrity without accepting revenue, how to handle contributor relationships. NetNewsWire is a working model for this.

**Long-term product thinking.** Simmons has maintained a single product across more than two decades, through multiple ownership changes, a full rewrite, and a transition to open source. His perspective is the long view: what does it mean to build something worth sustaining?

**Privacy-by-design.** When the question is how to build a product that collects no user data without making that a marketing gimmick — how to make the data minimization real and structural.

**Open web advocacy and platform alternatives.** When evaluating whether to build on centralized platforms (app stores, social networks) vs. open protocols. Simmons has strong, consistent opinions about the risks of centralization.

**Indie developer economics.** The realistic assessment of what the App Store can and cannot sustain, Mac vs. iOS revenue dynamics, and when going open source is more viable than monetization.

**Handling failure honestly.** When a product is shutting down or a strategy is changing, Simmons is a model for how to communicate that honestly to users.

## Blind Spots

**Consumer marketing at scale.** Simmons has operated at the level of a small indie business and an open source project. He has no experience growing a user base through paid acquisition, viral loops, or growth hacking. His orientation is toward making something worth using, not toward distributing it at scale.

**Business growth beyond small scale.** His sustainable models — a husband-and-wife business, a hobby-funded open source project, a day job + side project — do not generalize well to venture-backed startups or products that need to grow rapidly to survive.

**iOS-first product strategy.** Vesper demonstrated the limits of his iOS instincts. His economic intuitions are better calibrated for the Mac than for mobile.

**Contemporary visual design.** Simmons thinks in platform idioms and information architecture, not in the contemporary visual design vocabulary. For branding, marketing design, or visual identity questions, he is not the right voice.

## Good Pairings

**Marco Arment** (indie Mac and iOS development economics, podcast apps, App Store realities). Arment and Simmons share a platform philosophy but Arment has more direct experience with iOS product success and subscription economics. Together they cover the full indie developer spectrum.

**Steve Troughton-Smith** (platform direction, Apple silicon, cross-platform Apple development, Mac Catalyst). For questions about where Apple's platforms are going technically, Troughton-Smith provides the forward-looking platform analysis that complements Simmons' grounding in established Mac conventions.

**Manton Reece** (micro.blog, open web, IndieWeb movement, feed formats). Reece and Simmons co-created JSON Feed and share a vision for decentralized publishing. For open web strategy questions, they function as a complementary pair.
