# Brent Simmons — Quotes

Verified quotes from primary sources. Where a post title and date are known, they are cited. All quotes are from inessential.com or documented interviews unless otherwise noted.

---

> "Any piece of software should be describable in a sentence. NetNewsWire Lite is an RSS reader."

— Daring Fireball interview with Brent Simmons, March 2003 (daringfireball.net)

---

> "This is the big leagues, this is _the show_."

— On Mac development and user interface standards. Daring Fireball interview, March 2003

---

> "The Mac user interface is one of the great intellectual and artistic achievements of my lifetime."

— Interlude: Interview with Brent Simmons, Indie Microblogging (book.micro.blog)

---

> "It was always my personal dream to work on native Mac apps: I don't really love doing web UIs."

— Interlude: Interview with Brent Simmons, Indie Microblogging (book.micro.blog)

---

> "Your RSS reader should not be a taskmaster or a source of guilt."

— inessential.com, "NetNewsWire Article Age Limits," October 13, 2018

---

> "Make sure performance is part of the foundation of your app. Make sure it's part of every decision every day. Make sure, in other words, that performance isn't just a topping — it's the pizza."

— inessential.com, 2020 (via Six Colors, "Brent Simmons: Focus on what matters")

---

> "There is 100% trust that we never think about revenue when making decisions."

— inessential.com, "On Not Taking Money for NetNewsWire," February 20, 2023

---

> "The internet's town square should never have been one specific website."

— inessential.com, "After Twitter," November 13, 2022

---

> "The web is wide open again, for the first time in what feels like forever."

— inessential.com, "After Twitter," November 13, 2022

---

> "Corporations aren't people, and they can't love you back. You wouldn't love GE or Exxon or Comcast — and you shouldn't love Apple."

— inessential.com, "Corporations Are Not To Be Loved," January 17, 2024

---

> "The thing I've done the longest, love the most, and am most proud of is this blog."

— inessential.com, "Happy 20th to This Blog!" November 7, 2019

---

> "The best time to start a blog is 20 years ago. The second-best time is today."

— inessential.com, "Happy 20th to This Blog!" November 7, 2019

---

> "I believe that developers (particularly Mac and iOS developers, the group I know best) are so loath to work with XML that they won't even consider building software that needs an XML parser. Which says to me that JSON Feed is needed for the survival of syndication."

— inessential.com, "JSON Feed," May 17, 2017

---

> "I don't prize elegance as much as I value things that work well."

— inessential.com, "JSON Feed," May 17, 2017

---

> "if we had started with a Mac app rather than an iOS app, Vesper would have been much more successful."

— inessential.com, "More Notes on Vesper," August 21, 2016

---

> "It was time for me to return to my natural habitat as an indie developer."

— inessential.com, "Gone Indie," February 1, 2013

---

> "I'm not retiring from writing apps — which means I'll have a lot more time for working on NetNewsWire."

— inessential.com, "Retirement and NetNewsWire," May 24, 2025

---

> "The best innovation is often no innovation — present something new like RSS feeds in a familiar way."

— Rands in Repose interview with Brent Simmons (randsinrepose.com)

---

> "We don't want any private information. We really, really don't."

— NetNewsWire Privacy Policy (netnewswire.com/privacypolicy.html)

---

> "Everything I created while I was there is now somewhere else. Nothing of me remains there."

— inessential.com, "What Happened at NewsGator," May 10, 2014
