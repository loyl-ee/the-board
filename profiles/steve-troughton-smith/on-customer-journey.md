# Steve Troughton-Smith — On Customer Journey

## Real-Time Discovery as Community Service

Troughton-Smith's relationship with his audience is built around live discovery. When he finds something in Apple's firmware or toolchain, he posts about it immediately — on Mastodon (previously Twitter), with technical context, often within hours of the firmware or code drop becoming available. This is not press-release journalism; it is primary-source technical discovery shared in real time with other developers and informed observers.

The HomePod firmware discovery in July 2017 is the canonical example: he posted his findings on Twitter within hours of the firmware becoming available, the story was picked up by every major tech outlet within 24 hours, and Apple pulled the firmware. The speed and accuracy of his analysis — and his willingness to name specifically what he had found — gave him credibility that no amount of pundit commentary could replicate.

## Educating Developers on Platform Capabilities

Beyond the discovery work, a significant portion of his Mastodon and blog output is educational: explaining what Apple's frameworks can do, how to use underdocumented APIs, and how to navigate the gap between Apple's public documentation and what the platform actually supports. His Mac Catalyst sample code library on GitHub is the most tangible expression of this — a repository of working implementations of things Apple had documented poorly or not at all.

He has maintained this posture consistently: when Apple ships a new capability, he investigates and reports. When Apple ships a gap, he names it. When a workaround exists, he publishes it. The developer community around Apple platforms has come to treat his commentary as a reliable signal — if Troughton-Smith has posted about something, it is worth paying attention to.

## Patreon as Direct Support Model

His Patreon campaign funds the research, commentary, and community work that does not have a direct product revenue line. This is an honest direct-support relationship: patrons fund him specifically because they value the ongoing commentary and want it to continue. John Gruber's 2017 endorsement of his Patreon — calling him "extraordinarily generous about sharing what he learns with the world" — reflected the community's view accurately.

The Patreon model also preserves his independence. He is not sponsored by a company with interests in how Apple's platforms evolve, which means his criticism of Apple can be genuinely critical without commercial conflict of interest.

## Mastodon as Primary Channel

He migrated to Mastodon in late 2022 as one of the prominent figures in the tech developer community to leave Twitter. Developer Paul Hudson noted on X (formerly Twitter): "I really miss Steve Troughton-Smith on here, and I hope he comes back at some point. Whether it was building some really thoughtful apps, sharing his Catalyst code, or posting pictures of Warhammer work, he contributed a lot to this place." He has not returned, and Mastodon has become his primary real-time channel, where he has accumulated over 34,000 followers.
