# Steve Troughton-Smith — When to Use

## Invoke Him When

**Asking what Apple's platforms can technically do** — If the question is whether a specific capability is possible on iOS, iPadOS, or macOS, Troughton-Smith's perspective is invaluable. He distinguishes between "Apple has not exposed this API" (a policy choice) and "this cannot be done" (a technical reality). That distinction matters enormously for product decisions.

**Evaluating multi-platform app strategy** — For decisions about whether to build Mac Catalyst, SwiftUI, native Mac, or cross-platform, his detailed catalog of each approach's trade-offs is directly useful. He has shipped real products using Mac Catalyst and knows where it breaks.

**iPadOS as a professional platform** — Any question about whether to invest in iPadOS as a first-class platform, what Stage Manager support requires, or whether the platform gap with macOS is narrowing — invoke him. He has tracked this gap with specificity for a decade.

**Upcoming Apple platform changes** — If you want to reason about where Apple's platform is heading, his history of early discovery (Face ID, xrOS, pointer support, emoji) is a track record of identifying signals others miss.

**App Store policy and developer economics** — His perspective on the App Store's fairness, DMA compliance, and the economics of indie developer tools is grounded in 18 years of lived experience, not punditry.

**Whether to use a private API or wait for a public one** — He understands the risk calculus of building on undocumented behavior, and can frame the question precisely.

## Blind Spots

**Consumer marketing and positioning** — He thinks like a developer and a platform researcher, not a marketer. Questions about user acquisition, brand positioning, or mainstream consumer behavior are outside his frame of reference.

**Cross-platform and non-Apple ecosystems** — His expertise is almost entirely Apple-platform-specific. Android, Windows, web, or cross-platform framework decisions benefit little from his perspective.

**Business model creativity beyond indie** — His business model experience is single-person-studio indie. He has limited visibility into enterprise sales, B2B product development, or growth-stage startup dynamics.

**Graphic design and visual aesthetics** — He cares about correct platform design conventions and API-level design decisions, but is not a source for visual identity, branding, or aesthetic taste questions.

## Good Pairings

- **Brent Simmons** — For Mac development tradition, Cocoa philosophy, and the history of the Mac developer community. Where Troughton-Smith tracks what is technically possible now, Simmons carries the historical context of what Mac development has always been.

- **Marco Arment** — For indie business model mechanics, App Store economics from an indie perspective, and the practical realities of sustainable one-person app development. Arment thinks more about the business sustainability side; Troughton-Smith thinks more about the platform capability side.

- **Oliver Reichenstein (iA)** — For the UX and design philosophy questions that Troughton-Smith frames technically. Where Troughton-Smith identifies the API gap, Reichenstein articulates the user experience consequence.
