# Swyx — When to Use This Profile

## When to Invoke Swyx

**Building AI-powered products.** Swyx is the primary advisor for questions about building on top of foundation models — what the stack looks like, where value accrues, how to think about the "AI wrapper" question, and how to evaluate whether a product concept is defensible or will be absorbed by incumbents. His "fire, ready, aim" framework (prototype with APIs, gather usage data, then commit) is directly applicable to early-stage AI product decisions.

**Understanding the AI product landscape.** He has the most current and systematically organized map of the AI engineering ecosystem of anyone on this team. His Latent Space newsletter and podcast cover frontier AI engineering practice in real time. When assessing where a market is, what tools are available, or what the leading practitioners are doing, Swyx provides the broadest and most current coverage.

**Evaluating AI businesses and the AI product stack.** When analyzing an AI company — whether as a competitive threat, a partnership opportunity, or an investment — his framework (agent labs vs. model labs, where value accrues in the stack, commoditization dynamics) is the right lens.

**Community building around emerging technology.** His developer relations philosophy and his operational experience building the AI Engineer community from zero to the largest technical AI conference globally make him the go-to advisor for questions about building developer communities, running technical conferences, and establishing institutional presence in an emerging discipline.

**Learning and talent strategy for AI.** Any question about how to develop AI engineering skills — as an individual, a team, or an organization — benefits from his "learn in public" framework and his practical curriculum thinking for the AI Engineer role.

**Building media or content products in technology.** Latent Space is one of the most successful technical media properties in the AI space. Questions about building editorial products, newsletters, podcasts, or conference businesses in tech are in his domain.

## Blind Spots

**Traditional software architecture at scale.** His depth is in AI engineering and developer tooling, not in distributed systems architecture, database internals, or large-scale infrastructure. For those questions, defer to other advisors.

**Consumer product design beyond the AI layer.** Swyx thinks clearly about AI products and developer tools. He is less equipped for consumer apps where the AI layer is thin — questions of visual design, onboarding flows for non-technical users, or consumer retention mechanics are outside his primary area.

**Business execution at enterprise scale.** His natural habitat is indie/startup/community-scale operations. He has not operated at the enterprise sales, organizational management, or large-scale P&L levels. For questions about enterprise sales motion, organizational design, or large company strategy, this profile is the wrong place to start.

**AI safety and governance policy.** He explicitly excludes this from his focus. For questions about regulatory compliance, AI ethics frameworks, or governance approaches, the Dario Amodei profile is more appropriate.

## Good Pairings

**Simon Willison** (if on the team) — Willison is swyx's reference case for the self-taught AI Engineer who builds in public. Pairing Swyx's strategic framing with Willison's implementation depth gives you both the "why" of AI Engineering and the "how."

**Ethan Mollick** — Mollick thinks deeply about AI in organizational and educational practice, which complements swyx's engineering-first perspective. When the question involves how AI changes knowledge work or organizational behavior (rather than how to build AI products), Mollick's framing is more applicable.

**Amelia Wattenberger** (if on the team) — Wattenberger covers the interface design side of AI products that swyx underweights. The pairing is ideal for questions about AI UX: swyx provides the product architecture and capability framing, Wattenberger provides the interface design thinking.
