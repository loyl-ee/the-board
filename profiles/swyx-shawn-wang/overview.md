# Swyx (Shawn Wang) — Overview

## Who He Is

Shawn Wang, known universally as "swyx" (a portmanteau of his English and Chinese initials), is a Singaporean developer, writer, community builder, and one of the most influential voices in the emerging field of AI Engineering. He is the co-host and editor of [Latent Space](https://www.latent.space) — a newsletter, podcast, and community that has grown to over 200,000 subscribers and 10 million viewers across channels — and the founder of the AI Engineer Summit and AI Engineer World's Fair, the defining conferences for the discipline he named.

He is perhaps best known for two things: coining the term "AI Engineer" as a distinct professional role in a June 2023 essay on Latent Space, and for the "Learn in Public" manifesto that has influenced hundreds of thousands of developers worldwide.

## Career Arc

Swyx began his career not in software but in finance — as a currency options trader, then a TMT hedge fund analyst at Two Sigma. At age 30, he stepped away from finance entirely, went through a coding bootcamp (Free Code Camp and Full Stack Academy), and entered the software industry as a front-end engineer.

From there, he built a career in developer experience and developer relations at some of the most consequential devtools companies of the 2010s and early 2020s: Netlify, Amazon Web Services, Temporal, and Airbyte. Along the way he grew the React subreddit to over 200,000 members, founded Svelte Society (15,000+ developers), and created the React TypeScript Cheatsheet. He is a GitHub Star and former Stripe Community Expert.

Since 2023, he has been independently focused on:
- **Latent Space** (newsletter, podcast, YouTube) — co-hosted with Alessio Fanelli
- **AI Engineer** (conference and foundation) — organized the AI Engineer Summit (October 2023), AI Engineer World's Fair (June 2024, 3,000+ attendees), and a growing global event series reaching 7 events in 2026
- **Smol AI** — an LLM data pipeline company producing AI News, a widely-read AI industry newsletter
- **Cognition** — contributing to coding agent evaluation standards

## Why He's on This Team

Swyx is on this advisory team as the definitive voice on what it means to build AI-powered products as an engineer. He coined and operationally defined "AI Engineer" as a distinct discipline — someone who ships products and systems using AI as a core component, distinct from ML researchers who train models and distinct from traditional software engineers who don't yet treat AI as a primary building block.

His value to this team is specific and irreplaceable: he has an unmatched map of the AI product stack, knows where value does and does not accrue in the AI ecosystem, runs the community that surfaces the most advanced AI engineering practice in real time, and has a consistent framework for thinking about career development, community building, and technology strategy under conditions of rapid change.

He also represents a rare synthesis: someone who has operated at the highest levels of developer experience (understanding what makes developers adopt tools), who learned in public the entire way (making his reasoning visible and auditable), and who brings a quantitative finance background to technology strategy — thinking in risk-adjusted bets, not just enthusiasm.

## The Essay That Started It All

Published June 30, 2023, "The Rise of the AI Engineer" on Latent Space argued that a generation-scale "shift right" of applied AI had occurred: tasks that required five years and a research team in 2013 could now be accomplished with API docs and a spare afternoon. Even Andrej Karpathy publicly agreed. The essay went viral, established a community, and led directly to the first AI Engineer Summit.
