# Quotes — Mattt Thompson

Quotes below are drawn from verifiable sources: the Heroku interview (2013), NSHipster articles with known publication dates, and the NSHipster book. Quotes marked with article titles are from nshipster.com posts; dates are publication dates from the NSHipster article archive.

---

> "Code costs nothing to share, creates good will, and contributes to the gift economy. What's not to like?"

— Heroku interview ("The Incredibly True Story of Why an iOS Developer Dropped His CS Classes and Eventually Learned How to Fly"), November 2013

---

> "When you make something cool, you should show it off and share it with others."

— Heroku interview, November 2013

---

> "What really keeps me active in the community is the opportunity to make things that make the lives of others better, no matter how niche the audience or marginal the improvement."

— Heroku interview, November 2013

---

> "Computer Science classes never caught my passion like this, so I decided to drop out of the program, pursuing undergraduate degrees in Philosophy, Linguistics, and Art."

— Heroku interview, November 2013

---

> "Code structure and organization is a matter of pride for developers. Clear and consistent code signifies clear and consistent thought."

— "Swift Documentation," NSHipster, May 5, 2015

---

> "Everything you need to succeed as a software developer extends from a conscious practice of empathy."

— "Empathy," NSHipster, March 17, 2014

---

> "NSPredicate is truly one of the jewels of Cocoa. Other languages would be lucky to have something with half its capabilities in a framework — let alone the standard library."

— NSHipster: Obscure Topics in Cocoa & Objective C (book), 2013

---

> "Key-Value Observing has the worst API in all of Cocoa."

— NSHipster: Obscure Topics in Cocoa & Objective C (book), 2013

---

> "The Principle of Least Surprise describes a general approach to software that celebrates sanity over cleverness: clear intent, reasonable defaults, and control."

— NSHipster: Obscure Topics in Cocoa & Objective C (book), 2013

---

> "Each question is a data point for what could be clarified or improved."

— "Stewardship," NSHipster, January 27, 2014

---

> "The Swift Standard Library is on a collision course with Cocoa, and if the new language continues to gain momentum, one should expect to see further fracturing and reinvention within the system frameworks."

— "The Death of Cocoa," NSHipster, December 29, 2014

---

> "Standard libraries should implement standards."

— "The Death of Cocoa," NSHipster, December 29, 2014

---

> "Lacking many of the faculties to humanize and understand one another (facial expressions, voice tonality, non-verbal cues) we can lose sight of who we're talking to, and become less human ourselves."

— "Empathy," NSHipster, March 17, 2014
