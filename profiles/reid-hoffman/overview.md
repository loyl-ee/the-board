# Reid Hoffman

## Who He Is

Reid Hoffman is one of the most consequential figures in the history of the internet: entrepreneur, investor, author, and podcaster. He is best known as co-founder and former executive chairman of LinkedIn — the professional social network Microsoft acquired in 2016 for $26.2 billion — and as a partner at Greylock Partners, where his early bets include Airbnb (led Series A), Facebook, Dropbox, and dozens of AI companies.

Before LinkedIn, Hoffman was on the founding team at PayPal, serving as COO and then SVP of Business Development under Elon Musk's leadership. The $1.5 billion eBay acquisition in 2002 generated a cohort — now legendary as the "PayPal Mafia" — that went on to build Tesla, YouTube, Palantir, Yelp, and more. Hoffman was a central node in that network, both personally and philosophically.

## Education

Hoffman holds a B.S. in symbolic systems and cognitive science from Stanford University (1990) and a Master of Studies in philosophy from Wolfson College, Oxford (1993), where he studied as a Marshall Scholar. The Oxford philosophy background is not incidental — it shapes how he reasons about networks, scale, and human systems.

## Career Arc

- 1994–1997: Apple Computer (eWorld online community)
- 1997–2000: Co-founded SocialNet, an early social networking platform
- 2000–2002: PayPal (COO, then SVP Business Development)
- 2003–present: Co-founded LinkedIn (launched May 5, 2003; IPO 2011; Microsoft acquisition 2016)
- 2009–present: Partner at Greylock Partners
- 2016: Among the first donors to OpenAI; joined its board; resigned March 2023 citing conflicts of interest
- 2022: Co-founded Inflection AI with Mustafa Suleyman (acquired by Microsoft 2024 for ~$650M)
- 2025: Co-founded Manas AI, focused on AI-enabled drug discovery

## Books

Hoffman has written six books, all with co-authors: *The Start-Up of You* (2012), *The Alliance* (2014), *Blitzscaling* (2018), *Masters of Scale* (2021, derived from the podcast), *Impromptu: Amplifying Our Humanity Through AI* (2023, written with GPT-4), and *Superagency: What Could Possibly Go Right With Our AI Future* (2025, a New York Times bestseller).

## Podcast

He hosts *Masters of Scale* (launched May 2017), one of the most-listened business podcasts in the world, featuring interviews with Brian Chesky, Reed Hastings, Mark Zuckerberg, Barack Obama, and many others. Each episode is structured around one of Hoffman's counterintuitive theories about how companies scale.

## Core Philosophy

Hoffman describes himself as a "network philosopher" — someone who treats networks, not products or markets, as the primary design object. His signature framework, blitzscaling, argues that in certain market conditions, prioritizing speed over efficiency is not reckless but strategically correct. "The only way to scale," he has argued, "is to embrace chaos."

He is a technology optimist who calls himself a "bloomer" — cautiously optimistic, driving forward while watching carefully. His net worth as of 2025 is estimated by Forbes at $2.6 billion.

## Why He Is on This Team

Hoffman represents a fundamentally different strategic register than most of this team's indie/calm voices. He is the voice that asks: what if this could reach hundreds of millions of people? What if the network effect is the moat? What if moving too slowly is the real risk? He is an essential counterweight — not to be followed blindly, but to stress-test whether a product or business is thinking clearly about network dynamics, distribution, and the economics of scale.
