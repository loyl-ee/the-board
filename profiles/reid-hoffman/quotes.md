# Reid Hoffman — Quotes

All quotes below are from verifiable sources. Sources are cited inline.

---

> "If you aren't embarrassed by the first version of your product, you shipped too late."

— Reid Hoffman (widely attributed; repeated across interviews, Masters of Scale episodes, and Tim Ferriss Show appearances; first documented in Silicon Valley usage ~2011)

---

> "An entrepreneur is someone who will jump off a cliff and assemble an airplane on the way down."

— Reid Hoffman (widely attributed; repeated in interviews and public talks throughout his career)

---

> "Blitzscaling is a strategy and set of techniques for driving and managing extremely rapid growth that prioritize speed over efficiency in an environment of uncertainty."

— *Blitzscaling*, Reid Hoffman and Chris Yeh, 2018

---

> "If you win, efficiency isn't important; if you lose, efficiency is irrelevant."

— *Blitzscaling*, Reid Hoffman and Chris Yeh, 2018

---

> "Brilliant thinking is rare, but courage is in even shorter supply than genius."

— *Blitzscaling*, Reid Hoffman and Chris Yeh, 2018

---

> "Sizing the market for a disruptor based on an incumbent's market is like sizing a car industry off how many horses there were in 1910."

— *Blitzscaling*, Reid Hoffman and Chris Yeh, 2018

---

> "Culture is actually a substitute for bureaucracy and rules."

— *Blitzscaling*, Reid Hoffman and Chris Yeh, 2018

---

> "The fundamental paradox of the tour of duty: acknowledging that the employee might leave is actually the best way to build trust, and thus develop the kind of relationship that convinces great people to stay."

— *The Alliance: Managing Talent in the Networked Age*, Reid Hoffman, Ben Casnocha, and Chris Yeh, 2014

---

> "There are more smart people outside your company than inside it."

— *The Alliance: Managing Talent in the Networked Age*, Reid Hoffman, Ben Casnocha, and Chris Yeh, 2014

---

> "I'll make the case that the only way an organization can truly scale is to first do things that don't scale at all."

— Masters of Scale, Episode 1 (with Brian Chesky), May 2017

---

> "I have a theory that the best business ideas often seem laughable at first glance."

— Masters of Scale, Episode 3 (theory framing), 2017

---

> "Dream big. And act small. Pay passionate attention to your users. Handcraft the core service for them. Create a magical experience. And then figure out what part of that magical handcrafted thing can scale."

— Tim Ferriss Show, "Reid Hoffman and Brian Chesky: How to Scale to 100M+ Users" (#326), July 2018

---

> "You don't start with 100 million users. You start with a few. So stop thinking big, and start thinking small. Hand serve your customers. Win them over, one by one."

— Tim Ferriss Show, "Reid Hoffman and Brian Chesky: How to Scale to 100M+ Users" (#326), July 2018

---

> "There's so much dialogue about risks of all sorts. Yes, we need to pay attention — and be in the dialogue about — the risks, but the real important thing is to not fumble the future."

— Fortune, Reid Hoffman interview, December 2023

---

> "For many people 'twenty years of experience' is really one year of experience repeated twenty times."

— *The Startup of You*, Reid Hoffman and Ben Casnocha, 2012

---

> "A good product with great distribution will almost always beat a great product with poor distribution."

— Reid Hoffman, NFX interview with James Currier, "Reid Hoffman's Playbook for Growth and Network Effects"

---

> "The long-term value of LinkedIn was always intended to come from network effects."

— Reid Hoffman, NFX interview with James Currier, "Reid Hoffman's Playbook for Growth and Network Effects"

---

> "Network effects both produce and require aggressive growth."

— Reid Hoffman, NFX interview with James Currier, "Reid Hoffman's Playbook for Growth and Network Effects"
