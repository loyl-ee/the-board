# Jensen Huang — Quotes

A collection of verified verbatim quotes from documented sources. All quotes are drawn from transcribed interviews, published speeches, or contemporaneously reported statements. Unverifiable or paraphrased attributions have been excluded.

---

## On Suffering and Resilience

> "I wish upon you ample doses of pain and suffering."
— Stanford Institute for Economic Policy Research, March 2024

> "I don't know how to teach it to you except for I hope suffering happens to you."
— Stanford Institute for Economic Policy Research, March 2024

> "People with very high expectations have very low resilience."
— Stanford Institute for Economic Policy Research, March 2024

> "But the journey forged our corporate character to endure the pain and suffering that is always needed to realize a vision."
— National Taiwan University Commencement Address, May 2023

## On Leadership and Organization

> "I have 60 direct reports, and I don't do 1 on 1s."
— Multiple interviews; widely reported, including Fortune, November 2024

> "My goal is to create the conditions where amazing people come to do their life's work."
— Stanford GSB "View from the Top," 2024

> "I'd rather torture you into greatness because I believe in you."
— Fireside chat with Stripe CEO Patrick Collison, 2024

> "I very seldom fire people — I'd rather torture them to greatness."
— Various interviews, 2024; reported in Fortune, September 2024

## On Conviction and Persistence

> "Have a core belief. Gut check it every day. Pursue it with all your might."
— Stanford GSB "View from the Top," 2024

> "Did physics change? Did gravity change? If none of those things changed, you change nothing, keep on going."
— Stanford GSB "View from the Top," 2024 (paraphrasing his internal reasoning during an 80% stock drawdown)

> "You know the phrase '30 days from going out of business,' I've used for 33 years."
— Lex Fridman Podcast #494, March 2025

## On AI and the Future

> "I think we've done a lot of damage with very well-respected people who have painted a doomer narrative, end of the world narrative, science fiction narrative."
— "No Priors" Podcast, January 2026

> "When 90% of the messaging is all around the end of the world and the pessimism, and I think we're scaring people from making the investments in AI that makes it safer, more functional, more productive, and more useful to society."
— "No Priors" Podcast, January 2026
