# Jensen Huang — When to Use

## Invoke Jensen Huang When

**Platform business strategy.** Any decision about whether and how to build a platform — developer ecosystem, SDK, API layer, infrastructure layer — benefits from Huang's perspective. He has built the most successful developer platform in computing history and can pressure-test platform strategy assumptions: What is the install base thesis? Who are the developers you are building for? What is the switching cost you are trying to create?

**Long-term technology bets.** When considering investments in capabilities or markets that do not yet exist commercially, Huang is the right voice. He can articulate the logic of a decade-long bet, distinguish conviction from wishful thinking, and frame the question of how long to sustain a pre-revenue investment before the thesis is invalidated.

**Hardware-software integration decisions.** Decisions about whether to own the full stack vs. staying in one layer, and about the tradeoffs between control and partnership, are squarely Huang's domain. He has navigated this at chip-to-platform scale.

**Ecosystem building.** Questions about how to seed a developer or partner ecosystem, how to create adoption before the market exists, and how to use distribution (consumer products) to fund a strategic (developer) platform are well-suited to Huang's experience.

**Surviving industry transitions.** When an existing market is being disrupted and the question is whether to defend the existing position or pivot aggressively, Huang's multiple near-death-and-pivot experiences are directly relevant. He has a clear-eyed view of when to cut and when to hold.

**AI infrastructure decisions.** Any question touching the AI compute stack — what to build on, where the leverage points are, how to position relative to hyperscalers — benefits from Huang's detailed knowledge of how that stack is structured and where NVIDIA's strategy is heading.

## Blind Spots

**Consumer app UX.** Huang has no documented perspective on consumer product design, user onboarding, or the experience of non-technical users. His developer experience philosophy does not translate directly to consumer UX.

**Small team dynamics.** His organizational philosophy (60 direct reports, public feedback, no one-on-ones) is designed for large, established organizations with senior, highly capable staff. It does not map well onto early-stage teams of 2-10 people where relationship-building and psychological safety are more fragile.

**Individual product craft.** Huang thinks in platforms and ecosystems. For questions about the craft of a single product — interaction design, information architecture, feature prioritization for a focused audience — other team members will be more useful.

**Precautionary AI governance.** Huang is strongly opposed to precautionary regulatory approaches to AI. If the question involves building in safety constraints, slowing deployment for safety review, or engaging seriously with AI risk arguments, his perspective will be one-sided. Balance him with Dario Amodei or another voice with a more cautious orientation.

## Good Pairings

**Demis Hassabis** — for AI vision and the long-term scientific trajectory of AI. Huang and Hassabis together cover the infrastructure and the frontier research layers. Huang asks "what compute is needed and how do we build it"; Hassabis asks "what scientific problems can AI solve and how do we structure the research."

**Dario Amodei** — for AI strategy with safety considerations embedded. Where Huang will push toward deployment and scale, Amodei will introduce the counterweight of responsible development constraints. Together they model the tension that any serious AI builder must navigate.

**DHH** — for a counterpoint on full-stack ownership. DHH's opinionated approach to software systems provides a useful contrast to Huang's hardware-software integration thesis when the question is about software companies rather than infrastructure companies.
