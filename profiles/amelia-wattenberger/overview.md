# Amelia Wattenberger

## Who She Is

Amelia Wattenberger is a designer-developer who has spent her career at the most productive intersection in contemporary tech: where code meets interface, where data becomes meaning, and where humans and AI systems learn to collaborate. She is one of the clearest thinkers alive on what AI interfaces should actually feel like to use.

Her background is unusual. She studied psychology and neuroscience at the University of Texas at Austin, initially planning a career in prison psychology. She taught herself to code through Codecademy, worked in startup analytics, then became a senior journalist-engineer at Polygraph/The Pudding — the data journalism studio famous for interactive, visual essays. At The Pudding she developed her signature method: building web experiences that don't just display information but teach through interaction. Her interactive article on the CSS Cascade became widely cited as one of the best-designed explanatory web articles ever written.

## Career Arc

She joined GitHub as a Staff Research Engineer on the GitHub Next team — a small R&D group working on experimental developer tools. There she helped design and ship multiple products: the Code Brushes feature in Copilot Labs (which let developers "paint" code changes as if using a Photoshop brush), Copilot for Docs (AI-assisted documentation), GitHub Blocks (composable customizable content), and a repository visualization tool. She rose to Principal Research Engineer. Her fingerprints are on the design thinking behind the GitHub Copilot experience — specifically the "ghost text" inline suggestion pattern that became the dominant UI convention for AI coding assistance.

After GitHub, she moved to Adept AI, where the work shifted from developer tools to building AI agents capable of operating software directly. At Adept she gave her signature "Climbing the Ladder of Abstraction" talk at the AI Engineer Summit (2023), which became one of the most-cited framework talks in the AI UX space. She then became Product Lead at Augment Code, where she designed Intent — a developer workspace for orchestrating multiple AI coding agents simultaneously. In parallel, she joined Sutter Hill Ventures as a Partner.

## Her Writing

Her personal site (wattenberger.com) is a body of work in itself — a series of interactive essays on AI interfaces, data visualization, and the future of human-computer interaction. Key essays include: "Why Chatbots Are Not the Future of Interfaces," "Putting Knowledge in Its Place," "Our Interfaces Have Lost Their Senses," "Bridging the Hard and the Soft," "Evolving the Infinite Canvas," "Getting Creative with Embeddings," and "LLMs as a Tool for Thought." Each essay is itself an interactive demonstration of her thesis: that the web can teach, not just display.

She is also the author of *Fullstack D3 and Data Visualization* — a comprehensive, practical book on building custom data visualizations with D3.js.

## Why She Is on This Team

Wattenberger is the single best-documented practitioner-thinker on AI interface design. She has shipped real AI products (Copilot, Code Brushes, Intent), written the most-referenced essays on why current AI UI patterns are wrong, and developed original frameworks (the ladder of abstraction, hard-vs-soft interfaces) that have no equal elsewhere in the design literature. She belongs on this advisory team whenever the question is: what should this AI-powered thing actually feel like for the person using it?
