# David Smith — Overview

David Smith (known online as "Underscore" or @_DavidSmith) is an independent iOS developer based in Herndon, Virginia. He has been building apps for Apple platforms since the App Store launched to third-party developers in 2008, making him one of the earliest members of what would become the indie iOS development community. He is the creator of Widgetsmith, Pedometer++, Sleep++, Workouts++, Activity++, Watchsmith, Audiobooks, and more than 60 apps in total across his career.

## Career Arc

Smith was a web developer before Apple announced the iPhone SDK. He began building apps before he even owned an iPhone, developing in the simulator because he saw an opportunity and was unhappy with his consulting work. His first app was approved on November 8, 2008. His Audiobooks app, built in a single week in early 2009 to catalog the public domain LibriVox library, became his financial anchor — eventually downloaded by nearly 8 million people and allowing him to leave consulting behind entirely.

Over the following decade, Smith shipped app after app with minimal outside attention. He estimates that all but approximately six of his first 55-plus apps "completely failed." He describes this not as a tragedy but as the necessary cost of learning what the market actually wants — a lesson impossible to learn without shipping.

## The Widgetsmith Moment

In June 2020, Apple announced iOS 14's home screen widget system at WWDC. Smith had been working on Watchsmith, an Apple Watch complication customizer, and realized he could adapt the same SwiftUI code for iPhone widgets. He had roughly six weeks to build the app. Widgetsmith launched on September 16, 2020, two days after iOS 14. On September 18, a TikTok video by Katarina Mogus showing how to create an "aesthetic" iPhone home screen using Widgetsmith went viral. Smith described the moment on Twitter: "Widgetsmith just went viral on TikTok...which is a phrase I don't even really understand but is having a rather robust result on my life right now."

The app hit number one on the US App Store and held that position for over two weeks, surpassing Gmail, Netflix, and Spotify. It reached 50 million downloads by early 2021, 100 million by March 2023, and approximately 131 million by mid-2025. Every download came through word of mouth — Smith had no marketing budget and ran no ads for the app. Widgetsmith was the 59th app he had ever launched, built twelve years into a career most people had never heard of.

## Apps and Health Focus

Beyond Widgetsmith, Smith has sustained a distinct portfolio of health and fitness tracking apps for iPhone and Apple Watch: Pedometer++ (step counting and walking data, launched 2013), Sleep++ (automatic sleep tracking using Apple Watch motion sensors, launched 2015), Workouts++ (customizable Apple Watch workout displays), and Activity++ (activity streak and motivation tracking). These apps reflect both personal interest — Smith has written publicly about his three-year fitness journey and achieving a 300-pound deadlift — and a conviction that simple, privacy-respecting health tools have enduring value on Apple's platforms.

## Public Presence

Smith has been a prolific blogger at david-smith.org since 2011, writing about the economics of indie development, App Store dynamics, app design process, platform adoption statistics, and his personal experiences as a solo developer. He hosted the solo podcast Developing Perspective from 2011 to 2015 (episodes never longer than 15 minutes), then co-founded Under the Radar with Marco Arment on Relay FM in November 2015. Under the Radar ran for 331 episodes over ten years before ending in November 2025. He also maintains a public Design Diary documenting his design process in detail.

## Why He Is on This Team

David Smith replaced Chintan Patel as the indie iOS developer archetype on this team. Smith offers something more documented and more specific: a decade and a half of public writing about the actual economics, psychology, and craft of sustaining a solo app business on Apple's platform. He has experienced the full arc — obscurity, foundation income, unexpected viral success, scaling challenges, and long-term sustainability — and written honestly about all of it. When the question is how to build, price, ship, or sustain an iOS app as a solo or near-solo operation, David Smith is the most relevant voice on this team.
