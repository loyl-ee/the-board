# DHH — Quotes

These quotes are drawn from verified sources: published books, documented essays on Signal v. Noise and world.hey.com, recorded podcast transcripts, and official 37signals materials. Book quotes are co-authored with Jason Fried; individual attribution within those books is not always specified.

---

> "The best software has a vision. The best software takes sides."

— *Getting Real*, 37signals, 2006 (Chapter: Make Opinionated Software)

---

> "You're better off with a kick-ass half than a half-assed whole."

— *Rework*, Crown Business, 2010

---

> "Sustained exhaustion is not a badge of honor, it's a mark of stupidity."

— *It Doesn't Have to Be Crazy at Work*, HarperBusiness, 2018

---

> "The answer is not more hours, it's less bullshit."

— *It Doesn't Have to Be Crazy at Work*, HarperBusiness, 2018

---

> "We don't throw more people at problems, we chop problems down until they can be carried across the finish line by teams of three."

— *It Doesn't Have to Be Crazy at Work*, HarperBusiness, 2018

---

> "Forgoing sleep is like borrowing from a loan shark. Sure you get those extra hours right now to cover for your overly-optimistic estimation, but at what price?"

— "Sleep Deprivation Is Not a Badge of Honor," Signal v. Noise, 2008

---

> "Software development is rarely a sprint, but mostly a marathon. Multiple marathons, actually."

— "Sleep Deprivation Is Not a Badge of Honor," Signal v. Noise, 2008

---

> "Apple isn't just a dominant player in this market, they are the market. Without access to the iPhone, we're dead."

— "On Apple's Monopoly Power to Destroy HEY," Signal v. Noise, 2020

---

> "This is how you stifle entrepreneurship, depress innovation, and prevent the next generation of makers from ever having a chance to compete."

— "On Apple's Monopoly Power to Destroy HEY," Signal v. Noise, 2020

---

> "There is no chance in bloody hell that we're going to pay Apple's ransom."

— Interview/public statement on the HEY App Store dispute, June 2020 (reported by The Register and AppleInsider)

---

> "No comment thread on Basecamp is going to close the gap on fundamental philosophical and political differences."

— "Basecamp's New Etiquette Regarding Societal Politics at Work," world.hey.com/dhh, 2021

---

> "The term startup has been narrowed to describe the pursuit of total business domination."

— "RECONSIDER," Signal v. Noise / Medium, 2015

---

> "Designing for the web ought to mean making HTML and CSS. When that's getting too hard to do because of complexity sprawl of JavaScript, it's time to revisit your assumptions of how to work."

— DHH on Twitter/X, 2019

---

> "The price for pursuing JavaScript for everything has been a monstrosity of modern complexity."

— "HTML Over the Wire," Signal v. Noise, 2020

---

> "My favorite team size is three people. That's in my experience essentially the perfect team size."

— alphalist podcast interview (reported in alphalist.com blog)

---

> "I created Rails for me. To make me smile, first and foremost."

— DHH, various interviews; reflected in the Rails Doctrine (rubyonrails.org/doctrine)
