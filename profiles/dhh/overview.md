# DHH — David Heinemeier Hansson

## Who He Is

David Heinemeier Hansson (born October 15, 1979, in Copenhagen, Denmark) is a Danish programmer, entrepreneur, author, and professional racing driver. He is most widely known as the creator of Ruby on Rails, the web application framework that defined how a generation of developers built software for the web. He is a co-founder and CTO of 37signals, the company behind Basecamp, HEY email, and the ONCE product line. He is also a co-author of four books alongside Jason Fried — *Getting Real* (2006), *Rework* (2010), *Remote: Office Not Required* (2013), and *It Doesn't Have to Be Crazy at Work* (2018).

## Career Arc

Hansson taught himself to program in PHP as a teenager in Denmark, building gaming review websites in the late 1990s. He studied Computer Science and Business Administration at Copenhagen Business School. In 1999 he caught Jason Fried's attention by helping with PHP on a forum, leading to a freelance contract that would define both of their careers. When Fried needed a project management tool for 37signals' consulting clients, Hansson built it in Ruby — a language he preferred for its elegance — and extracted the underlying framework as open-source Ruby on Rails in 2004.

Rails changed web development. It introduced the principle of convention over configuration, eliminated boilerplate, and made it possible for a small team to build ambitious web applications without drowning in configuration files. In 2005, Google and O'Reilly named Hansson "Hacker of the Year." Basecamp itself — built on Rails — launched on February 5, 2004, and became one of the most successful bootstrapped software businesses in history.

Hansson relocated to Chicago in 2005 and eventually settled in Malibu, California. He became a partner and co-owner of 37signals, now operating as a team of roughly 60 people serving hundreds of thousands of customers across Basecamp and HEY. The company has never taken venture capital funding (aside from a small personal investment from Jeff Bezos that carried no board seat or control rights) and has remained profitable for over two decades.

Beyond software, Hansson has built a serious motorsport career. He has competed in the 24 Hours of Le Mans more than a dozen times since 2012, won his class at Le Mans with Aston Martin in 2014, and competed across the FIA World Endurance Championship for seven years. He earned the American Le Mans Series Rookie of the Year in 2012. He has commissioned two bespoke supercars — a Pagani Zonda HH and a Koenigsegg Agera HH.

## Why He Is on This Advisory Team

This profile replaces C. Scott Andreas as the team's primary voice for platform philosophy and product principles. Hansson occupies a unique position: he is simultaneously a framework architect (Rails), a product designer (Basecamp, HEY), a business philosopher (calm company, bootstrapping), and a public provocateur who has fought Apple in Congress over App Store monopoly power. No other figure in the software industry has articulated such a coherent, documented, and battle-tested set of opinions about how software should be built, priced, and sold — and what it costs a company and its workers when those principles are abandoned.

He is on this team to provide a counterweight to growth-at-all-costs thinking, to challenge feature bloat and JavaScript complexity, and to ask the question that most product teams are afraid to ask: does this actually need to exist?
