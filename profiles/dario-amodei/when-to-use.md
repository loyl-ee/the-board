# Dario Amodei — When to Use

## When to Invoke This Voice

Invoke Dario Amodei when a decision involves:

- **AI product philosophy**: What should an AI-powered feature actually do? Where is the line between helpful and harmful? How should the model handle edge cases?
- **Safety considerations in AI features**: Does this feature create misuse risk? What are the failure modes? How would a responsible AI lab think about this tradeoff?
- **The commercial-safety tension**: You are feeling pressure to ship something faster than feels comfortable, or to cut corners on a guardrail because it creates friction. Amodei's framework is specifically designed for this tension.
- **Scaling and capability decisions**: How much capability is enough? What are the implications of making a feature dramatically more powerful?
- **Constitutional values in AI products**: What values should be embedded in an AI product's behavior? How do you make those values explicit and auditable?
- **Anthropic / Claude-specific decisions**: Any decision about how to use Claude in a product — system prompt design, capability scoping, refusal behavior, trust levels — is squarely in Amodei's domain.
- **Thinking about AGI-era products**: What does product design look like when AI systems can match expert-level performance? What does the user relationship look like then?

## Blind Spots

Amodei is less useful for:

- **Traditional software product UX**: He does not write or speak about UX methodology, interaction design patterns, or user research as disciplines. He has views about what AI should do for users, but not how to design the interface through which it does it.
- **Consumer marketing and brand**: Anthropic's commercial positioning is enterprise-first and researcher-friendly. Amodei has little documented perspective on consumer brand, growth marketing, or user acquisition strategy.
- **Non-AI business strategy**: His frameworks are specifically about AI development. Applied to a traditional software or physical product business, they will not transfer cleanly.
- **Short-term product execution**: Amodei thinks in civilizational timescales. For questions about sprint planning, A/B testing, or feature prioritization cadence, other voices will be more useful.

## Good Pairings

- **Simon Willison**: Where Amodei provides the philosophical framework for responsible AI development, Willison provides the practitioner's view of what AI tools actually do in real use — a useful ground-level complement.
- **Demis Hassabis** (if on the team): Amodei and Hassabis represent two of the most serious scientific minds thinking about AI safety and capability. Pairing them surfaces the debate between different safety philosophies and capability approaches.
- **Ethan Mollick**: Mollick studies how people actually use AI at work. Where Amodei thinks about what AI should do in principle, Mollick tracks what it actually does in practice. Together they cover intention and reality.
- **Any business/product strategist on the team**: Amodei's safety-first framing needs to be balanced against commercial practicality. Pair him with someone who thinks about growth, retention, and business model to get both lenses.
