# Dario Amodei

## Who He Is

Dario Amodei (born 1983, San Francisco) is the co-founder and CEO of Anthropic, the AI safety company behind the Claude model series. He is among the most consequential figures in contemporary AI development — a researcher who helped establish some of the field's foundational scaling insights, and a leader who has staked his career on the thesis that safety-focused labs must be at the frontier of AI, not ceding that ground to others.

## Education and Early Career

Amodei began undergraduate studies at Caltech before transferring to Stanford University, where he earned a Bachelor of Science in physics in 2006. He completed a PhD in biophysics at Princeton University, studying electrophysiology of neural circuits — work for which he received the Hertz Foundation Doctoral Thesis Prize. He subsequently worked as a postdoctoral scholar at Stanford University School of Medicine, developing computational methods for high-throughput protein analysis.

His transition into AI began in earnest at Baidu (2014–2015), then Google, before joining OpenAI in 2016. At OpenAI he rose to Vice President of Research, overseeing development of GPT-2 and GPT-3. He is also recognized as a co-inventor of reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF), the technique that became central to making large language models useful.

## Founding Anthropic

In 2021, Amodei and his sister Daniela Amodei — along with several other senior OpenAI researchers — departed to found Anthropic. The split was driven by directional differences over how seriously to treat AI safety concerns. At Anthropic, Dario serves as CEO and Daniela as President. As of early 2026, Anthropic is valued at approximately $380 billion and has raised over $15 billion from investors including Amazon (up to $33 billion committed) and Google.

## Anthropic's Mission

Anthropic describes itself as an AI safety company. Its stated mission is "the responsible development and maintenance of advanced AI for the long-term benefit of humanity." The company occupies what Amodei calls a "peculiar position" — believing AI may be one of the most transformative and potentially dangerous technologies ever built, and building it anyway on the grounds that safety-focused organizations must be at the frontier rather than absent from it.

## Why He Is on This Team

Dario Amodei is the most articulate and rigorous thinker on what it means to build AI responsibly at scale. His long-form writing ("Machines of Loving Grace," "The Adolescence of Technology," "The Urgency of Interpretability") offers frameworks for thinking about AI's upside and downside that go far beyond typical tech optimism or safety doomerism. Because this knowledge system is built on Claude — an Anthropic model — his philosophy is literally embedded in the tool. Invoking him means asking: what does it mean to build AI products that are genuinely helpful, honest, and safe? What does the person most responsible for Claude think about the problems we are trying to solve?

## Recognition

Time magazine listed Amodei among its 100 Most Influential People in 2025 and named him an "Architect of AI" in its Person of the Year issue. Forbes estimated his net worth at $7 billion as of early 2026.
