# Oliver Reichenstein — Overview

## Who He Is

Oliver Reichenstein is a Swiss designer born in 1971 in Basel, Switzerland. He studied philosophy at universities in Basel and Paris before discovering the early web industry in the late 1990s. He is the founder and director of Information Architects (iA), a design studio with offices in Zurich and Tokyo (originally founded in Tokyo in 2005). He is best known as the creator of iA Writer, one of the most respected and influential writing apps ever made, and as a prolific essayist on typography, writing, and design philosophy through the iA blog at ia.net/topics.

## Career Arc

Reichenstein entered the web industry almost by accident. After studying philosophy, he began working as a copywriter at a startup, then spent four years as an interactive brand consultant at Interbrand Zintzmeyer&Lux, handling clients like Deutsche Telekom. Facing burnout, he took a bold break at age 33 and moved to Japan — originally for a vacation that became a years-long stay.

The Japan period was formative. Unable to read kanji, he was forced to experience writing as pure shape and form. As he described it: "When you can't read, you start looking at the shape of writing" — and he saw that what was missing from the web was exactly that kind of typographic care. He discovered the Swiss typographic masters — Frutiger, Ruder, Tschichold, Müller-Brockmann — not in Basel, but in Tokyo bookshops, "like discovering grammar after already being able to speak."

He founded iA (Information Architects Inc.) in Tokyo in 2005. The firm quickly became respected for redesigning major media institutions — Die Zeit, NHK, Wikipedia, Condé Nast, The Guardian, Red Bull, Monotype, Tages-Anzeiger — with an approach grounded in typography-first information architecture.

In 2010, iA released iA Writer for iPad. It was a revelation: a writing app that stripped away every formatting option and forced writers to focus on words alone. It sparked an entire category of focused writing tools. iA Writer went on to win four App of the Year awards and was nominated as an Apple Design Award Finalist in 2025.

iA has since expanded its product line to include iA Presenter (2022, a text-to-slides tool) and iA Notebook (2023, a physical paper notebook with watermark technology that won the Red Dot "Best of the Best" Award for Product Design 2025 — the first paper notebook to receive this distinction). In 2025, iA celebrated its 20th anniversary, and iA Writer turned 15.

## Why He Is on This Team

Reichenstein is on this advisory team as the definitive voice on writing tools, typographic design, and focused software. No other figure in the design world has thought as carefully, as publicly, or as philosophically about what it means to build software that helps people write and think. His views on typography as the foundation of communication, on removing distraction as a moral UX act, on AI and authorship, and on the business of staying small and principled are all directly applicable to decisions about writing tools, product philosophy, and the design of any interface where clarity of communication matters.

He represents a lineage connecting Swiss rationalism, Japanese minimalism, and the practical demands of digital product design — a rare combination.
